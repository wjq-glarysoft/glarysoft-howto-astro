If your Windows PC is running slow or acting strangely, performance troubleshooting is essential. Whether you’re a casual user or a tech enthusiast, identifying and resolving the causes of sluggishness can greatly improve your computing experience. Here are five proven methods to troubleshoot and boost Windows performance, focusing on Clean Up & Repair techniques.

What Causes Poor Windows Performance?

Before jumping into solutions, it’s important to understand why your PC might be running poorly. Common culprits include unnecessary startup programs, accumulated junk files, outdated drivers, malware, and fragmented disks. Addressing these areas will help restore your PC’s speed and efficiency.

Method 1: Remove Unnecessary Startup Programs

Beginner Guide: Many programs automatically start with Windows, which can slow boot time and consume resources.

Step-by-step: 1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Click the “Startup” tab. 3. Review the list and disable programs you don’t need at startup by right-clicking and selecting “Disable.”

Example: If you notice apps like Spotify or Skype loading every time you boot your PC, disable them here unless you use them daily.

Method 2: Clean Up Junk Files and Unwanted Programs

Beginner Guide: Temporary files, system cache, and leftover data from uninstalled programs can clutter your hard drive and slow down performance.

Step-by-step: 1. Open “Settings” and go to “System” > “Storage.” 2. Click on “Temporary files” and select items to delete. 3. Uninstall unused programs from “Apps & features.”

Example: Removing old games or trial software you no longer use frees up space and resources.

Method 3: Run a Malware and Virus Scan

Beginner Guide: Malware can dramatically slow down your PC by running malicious processes in the background.

Step-by-step: 1. Open Windows Security from the Start menu. 2. Click “Virus & threat protection” and then “Quick scan.” 3. Follow prompts to remove any threats found.

Example: Detecting and removing adware or browser hijackers that inject ads or redirect searches.

Advanced Tip: Glary Utilities includes a Malware Remover tool that can supplement your main antivirus, detecting potentially unwanted programs and browser plugins.

Method 4: Repair Corrupted System Files

Beginner Guide: Corrupted or missing system files can cause slowdowns or unusual errors.

Step-by-step: 1. Type “cmd” in the Start menu, right-click “Command Prompt,” and choose “Run as administrator.” 2. Type sfc /scannow and press Enter. 3. Let the scan complete and follow on-screen instructions to repair files.

Example: System File Checker can fix issues stemming from improper shutdowns or failed Windows updates.

Method 5: Defragment and Optimize Hard Drives

Beginner Guide: Fragmented files on traditional hard drives can slow down data access.

Step-by-step: 1. Press Windows + S and search for “Defragment and Optimize Drives.” 2. Select your drive and click “Optimize.”

Example: Defragmenting can improve load times for large files and games.
