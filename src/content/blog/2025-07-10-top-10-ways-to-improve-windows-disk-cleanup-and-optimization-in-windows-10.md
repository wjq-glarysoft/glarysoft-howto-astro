Keeping your Windows 10 system running smoothly often comes down to how well you maintain your disk health and overall performance. While the built-in Disk Cleanup tool provides basic cleaning functions, advanced techniques and third-party utilities can yield far better results. Here are the top 10 ways to boost disk cleanup and optimization on Windows 10, covering essential steps for both beginners and more experienced users.

Why Is Disk Cleanup and Optimization Important?

Over time, your Windows 10 PC accumulates junk files, unnecessary system files, and fragmented data. This can slow down your system, consume valuable storage space, and even cause software errors. Regular cleanup and optimization ensure your PC runs faster, remains secure, and delivers a better user experience.

For Beginners: Essential Cleanup and Optimization Steps

1\. Use Built-in Disk Cleanup Tool What is it? Windows includes a Disk Cleanup utility that targets temporary files, thumbnails, system files, and more.

How to use it: - Type "Disk Cleanup" in the Start menu and open it. - Select your system drive (usually C:). - Check categories you want to clean, like Temporary files, Recycle Bin, and Thumbnails. - Click OK and then Delete Files.

Tip: For deeper cleaning, click "Clean up system files" to remove old Windows updates and previous installations.

2\. Empty the Recycle Bin Regularly Why? Files in the Recycle Bin still occupy hard drive space until permanently deleted.

How to do it: - Right-click Recycle Bin on your desktop and select "Empty Recycle Bin." - Confirm the action to permanently remove files.

3\. Uninstall Unused Programs Unused programs take up disk space and can even run background processes that slow your system.

How to do it: - Go to Settings > Apps > Apps & features. - Browse the list, select programs you no longer use, and choose Uninstall.

4\. Manage Startup Programs Disabling unnecessary startup programs can improve boot time and free up system resources.

How to do it: - Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Go to the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable any non-essential programs.

5\. Remove Temporary System Files Automatically Windows 10 can automatically remove temporary files and content from the Downloads folder.

How to do it: - Go to Settings > System > Storage. - Turn on Storage Sense. - Configure how often you want Windows to delete temporary files.
