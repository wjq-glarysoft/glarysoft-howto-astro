What Are Windows Visual Effects and Why Do They Matter?

Windows visual effects include animations, shadows, fading menus, and other graphical enhancements designed to make the interface look sleek. While they improve the user experience visually, they can slow down your system, causing sluggishness during everyday tasks. Disabling or reducing some of these effects can lead to noticeable speed improvements, particularly on systems with limited RAM or older graphics hardware.

How Can You Manually Adjust Windows Visual Effects?

Windows provides built-in options to adjust visual effects. Here's how you can do it:

1\. Right-click on 'This PC' or 'Computer' on your desktop or in File Explorer. 2. Choose 'Properties'. 3. Click 'Advanced system settings' on the left sidebar. 4. Under the 'Performance' section, click 'Settings'. 5. In the 'Performance Options' window, you'll see a list of visual effects.

You have several choices: - Let Windows choose what’s best for my computer: Windows automatically selects effects based on your hardware. - Adjust for best appearance: All effects are enabled for the most visual appeal. - Adjust for best performance: All effects are disabled for maximum speed. - Custom: You can check or uncheck individual effects.

For most users looking to optimize speed, 'Adjust for best performance' is a good starting point. You can then enable a few effects you prefer, such as smooth edges on screen fonts for readability.
