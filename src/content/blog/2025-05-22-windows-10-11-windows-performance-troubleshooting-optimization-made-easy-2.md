Understanding how to maintain and optimize your Windows 10 or 11 PC can significantly enhance its performance and longevity. In this guide, we'll explore practical, actionable steps for troubleshooting and optimizing your system with a focus on cleanup and repair. Whether you're a beginner or an advanced user, you’ll find useful tips tailored to your skill level.

Why Is My PC Running Slow?

Before diving into solutions, it's important to understand why your computer might be running sluggishly. Common reasons include accumulated temporary files, startup program overload, malware infections, outdated drivers, and fragmented disks. Addressing these issues can lead to a more responsive and faster PC.

Getting Started: A Beginner's Guide

1\. Cleaning Up Unnecessary Files One of the simplest ways to improve performance is by removing unnecessary files that clutter your system.

\- Use Disk Cleanup: Access this tool by typing "Disk Cleanup" in the Windows search bar. Select the drive you want to clean, typically C:, and let the tool calculate how much space you can free up. Check items like temporary files and the recycle bin, then click "OK" to clean them.

\- Leverage Glary Utilities: For beginners, Glary Utilities offers an intuitive interface to clean junk files efficiently. Simply open the program, navigate to "1-Click Maintenance," and select "Junk Files Cleaner." Run the cleaner to remove clutter with just a few clicks.

2\. Managing Startup Programs Startup programs can slow down your boot time and overall system speed.

\- Disable Unnecessary Programs: Right-click the taskbar and select "Task Manager." Go to the "Startup" tab, review the list of programs, and disable those you don't need to start automatically.

\- Glary Utilities Startup Manager: This feature provides a more detailed view and allows you to safely manage startup entries. Access it under “Advanced Tools” and choose “Startup Manager” to enable or disable items as needed.

3\. Keeping Your System Updated Ensuring your system is up to date is crucial for performance and security.

\- Windows Update: Navigate to "Settings" > "Update & Security" > "Windows Update" and click "Check for updates." Install any available updates to keep your system running smoothly.

Advanced Users: Taking Optimization Further

1\. Registry Cleaning While beginners might do well to avoid registry adjustments, advanced users can benefit from cleaning up the registry for enhanced performance.

\- Built-in Defrag Tool: Type "Defragment and Optimize Drives" in the search bar, select your HDD, and click "Optimize."
