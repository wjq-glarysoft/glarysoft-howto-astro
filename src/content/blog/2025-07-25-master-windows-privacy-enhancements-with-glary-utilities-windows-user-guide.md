Why Does Windows Privacy Matter?

When you use a Windows PC, your device stores personal information, browsing history, system logs, and even sensitive credentials. Many apps and features collect telemetry data by default. If left unmanaged, this data can be vulnerable to misuse, hacking, or unwanted sharing. Enhancing your privacy not only keeps your data secure but also improves system performance by reducing unnecessary background activity.

Intermediate & Advanced Section: Deep-Dive Privacy Controls

How can I secure my PC beyond basic settings?

\- Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback. - Set Diagnostic Data to 'Required only' (or 'Basic' on some versions). - Turn off Tailored experiences and Feedback frequency.
