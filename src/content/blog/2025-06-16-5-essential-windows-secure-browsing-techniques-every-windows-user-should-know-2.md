Staying safe online is no longer optional—it’s a necessity for every Windows user. Cyber threats are evolving, and your personal information, browsing habits, and even your identity could be at risk if you don’t take proactive steps. Fortunately, Windows provides robust security features, and you can further enhance your protection with some smart habits and tools. Here are five essential secure browsing techniques that every Windows user should know, along with practical examples and step-by-step instructions to keep your online experience safe and private.

Why Should You Use a Modern Browser and Keep It Updated?

The browser is your main gateway to the internet—and often, the first target for cybercriminals. Outdated browsers can have vulnerabilities that attackers exploit. Always use a reputable, up-to-date browser such as Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, or Mozilla Firefox.

How to Do It: - Enable automatic updates in your browser settings. Most modern browsers update automatically, but it’s wise to check manually every now and then. - For Microsoft Edge: 1. Click the three-dot menu in the top right. 2. Go to Help and feedback > About Microsoft Edge. 3. The browser will check for updates and install them if available. - For Chrome: 1. Click the three-dot menu > Help > About Google Chrome.

Best practice: Restart your browser when prompted to apply updates immediately, ensuring you are always protected.

How Can You Strengthen Privacy with Browser Security Settings?

Your browser's default settings often prioritize convenience over privacy. Adjusting these settings can help block trackers, malicious sites, and unwanted downloads.

Step-by-Step Example: - In Edge: 1. Go to Settings > Privacy, search, and services. 2. Set the tracking prevention level to “Strict.” 3. Turn on “Microsoft Defender SmartScreen” for protection against malicious sites/downloads. - In Chrome: 1. Go to Settings > Privacy and security. 2. Enable “Safe Browsing (Enhanced protection)” for increased security. 3. Review and clear cookies and site data regularly.

Tip: Regularly clear your browsing data to remove traces of your online activity. Glary Utilities can help automate this process with its “Tracks Eraser” feature, which cleans browser cache, cookies, and history from multiple browsers in just one click.

What Role Do Password Managers and Multi-Factor Authentication Play?

Strong, unique passwords are essential for every online account. Never reuse passwords, and always enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) where possible to add an extra layer of security.

How to Apply This: - Use a trusted password manager to generate and store complex passwords. Many browsers have built-in password managers, but third-party tools offer more features. - For MFA: 1. Go to your account’s security settings (e.g., Microsoft, Google). 2. Follow prompts to add a secondary method, such as a phone app or SMS.

How Can You Recognize and Avoid Phishing Attacks?

Phishing is a common way hackers try to steal your information. Learn to spot suspicious emails, links, and websites.

Actionable Steps: - Always check the sender’s email address and hover over links to see their true destination. - Never download attachments or click on links from unknown sources. - Use Microsoft Defender SmartScreen (built into Edge and Windows) to warn about dangerous sites.

Practical Example: If you receive an email stating your bank account is compromised, don’t click any links in the message. Instead, directly visit your bank’s official website by typing the address in your browser.
