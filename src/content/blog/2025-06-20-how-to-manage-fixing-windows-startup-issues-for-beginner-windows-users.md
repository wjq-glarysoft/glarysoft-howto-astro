What are Windows Startup Issues and Why Do They Happen?

Windows startup issues occur when your computer fails to load Windows properly. You might see a blank screen, error messages, or your PC might keep restarting. Causes range from corrupted system files, recently installed updates, faulty drivers, or even malware.

Beginner Section: Simple Steps to Fix Windows Startup Problems

1\. Restart Your Computer Sometimes, a startup glitch can be solved with a simple restart. Hold down the power button until your PC turns off, wait a few seconds, then turn it on again.

2\. Disconnect External Devices External devices like USB drives or printers can sometimes interfere with startup. Remove all devices except for your keyboard and mouse, then restart your computer.

3\. Use Windows Startup Repair Windows includes a built-in Startup Repair tool that can automatically fix many problems.

How to Run Startup Repair: - Turn your PC off and on three times. On the third turn, Windows should enter the Recovery Environment. - Select Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Startup Repair. - Follow the on-screen instructions.

4\. Boot into Safe Mode Safe Mode starts Windows with only the essential files and drivers.

How to Enter Safe Mode: - Repeatedly turn your PC off and on (as above) until you see the Recovery menu. - Go to Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Startup Settings > Restart. - After restart, press 4 or F4 to start in Safe Mode.

If your PC starts in Safe Mode, a recently installed app or update might be causing the problem.

Advanced Section: Deeper Fixes for Persistent Startup Issues

If the above steps don’t solve the problem, these advanced options can help.

1\. Restore Your System to a Previous Point

System Restore can roll back your PC to a time before issues started.

\- Boot into the Windows Recovery Environment (as above). - Select Troubleshoot > Advanced options > System Restore. - Follow the on-screen steps to restore your computer to an earlier state.

2\. Scan and Repair Windows System Files

Corrupted system files can prevent Windows from starting.

\- Boot into Safe Mode or the Recovery Environment. - Open Command Prompt (Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Command Prompt). - Type sfc /scannow and press Enter.

This will check for and repair corrupted system files.

If your computer still won’t start after following these steps, it might have hardware issues or severe system corruption. In this case, contact a professional technician or your PC manufacturer’s support.
