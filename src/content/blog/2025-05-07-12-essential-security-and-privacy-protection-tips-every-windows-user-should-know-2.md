As Windows users, ensuring your system's security and privacy is paramount. Whether you're using your PC for work, personal tasks, or entertainment, safeguarding your data against cyber threats is crucial. Here are twelve essential tips that every intermediate Windows user should implement.

1\. How Do You Keep Windows Updated? Regular updates are critical for maintaining security. Windows Update automatically installs security patches, but it's wise to manually check for updates periodically. Navigate to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update and click on 'Check for updates' to ensure your system is current.

2\. Why is a Strong Password Important? Using strong, unique passwords is your first line of defense. Combine uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider using a password manager to keep track of complex passwords securely.

3\. Should You Enable Two-Factor Authentication? Yes, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA) on your accounts adds an extra layer of security. Even if your password is compromised, the additional authentication step protects you. Microsoft accounts and many services like Gmail or Dropbox support 2FA.

4\. How Can Windows Defender Enhance Security? Windows Defender offers robust protection against malware and viruses. Ensure it's enabled and conduct periodic scans. Navigate to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Security > Virus & threat protection to manage your security settings.

5\. What is Firewall Configuration? A properly configured firewall protects your system from unauthorized access. Windows Firewall is typically enabled by default, but you can check its status in Control Panel > System and Security > Windows Defender Firewall. Ensure it's active to block potentially harmful traffic.

6\. Why Use a VPN? A VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your internet traffic, securing data from prying eyes, especially on public Wi-Fi. There are many reliable VPN services available that can be easily set up on Windows to enhance online privacy.

[Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) offers privacy protection by cleaning up traces of your internet activity and secure deletion of files. Utilize its 'Privacy & Security' tools to remove tracking cookies, browser history, and securely delete sensitive files to prevent unauthorized recovery.

8\. Can User Account Control (UAC) Improve Security? Enable UAC to prevent unauthorized changes to your system. It alerts you when a program tries to make changes, giving you the option to allow or deny. You can adjust UAC settings through Control Panel > User Accounts > Change User Account Control settings.

9\. How Do You Manage App Permissions? Review which apps have access to your data and permissions they don't need. Go to Settings > Privacy and navigate through tabs like Location, Camera, and Microphone to manage app permissions effectively.

10\. What is BitLocker and Should You Use It? BitLocker encrypts your drive, protecting your data from theft or unauthorized access. Available on Pro and Enterprise versions, enable BitLocker through Control Panel > System and Security > BitLocker Drive Encryption.

11\. How to Secure Your Network? Changing the default login credentials for your router and using a strong Wi-Fi password are basic yet effective measures. Additionally, consider hiding your SSID (network name) and enabling network encryption (WPA3 if available).

12\. Why Should You Regularly Backup Data? Regular backups protect against data loss due to malware or hardware failure. Use Windows built-in Backup & Restore tool or third-party applications to schedule regular backups to an external drive or cloud storage.

Implementing these practices will significantly improve your Windows system's security and privacy. By staying proactive and using tools like Glary Utilities, you can enhance your protection against potential threats, ensuring a safer computing environment.
