Windows 10 users often turn to built-in tools like Disk Cleanup and optimization utilities to keep their systems running smoothly. Why Is Disk Cleanup and Optimization Important?

Over time, Windows accumulates unnecessary files: temporary files, update leftovers, logs, and more. These can consume valuable disk space and slow down your PC. Regular disk cleanup and optimization not only free up space but also improve system responsiveness and prolong hardware life.

What Are the Most Common Mistakes in Disk Cleanup?

Mistake 1: Deleting Essential Files

Many users mistakenly select all options in the Windows Disk Cleanup utility without understanding their impact. For example, clearing the "Windows ESD Installation Files" can prevent you from resetting or refreshing your PC in the future.

Solution: Before running Disk Cleanup, review each category. Only delete files you are sure you no longer need. Hover over each item for a description or research unfamiliar options.

Mistake 2: Forgetting to Empty the Recycle Bin

The Recycle Bin can accumulate gigabytes of deleted files. Many overlook emptying it, missing out on a quick way to reclaim space.

Solution: Always include the Recycle Bin in your cleanup routine. Alternatively, use Glary Utilities, which highlights space occupied by the Recycle Bin and allows you to clear it with one click.

Mistake 3: Skipping System Restore and Shadow Copies

Some users delete all but the most recent restore point without considering the need to roll back to an earlier system state. Removing these too aggressively can eliminate your safety net.

Solution: Keep at least one or two restore points, especially before making major changes.

Mistake 4: Not Optimizing (Defragmenting) Regularly

While Windows 10 automatically schedules disk optimization, users may disable this feature or forget to check SSD vs HDD requirements. Running defragmentation on an SSD can reduce its lifespan.

Solution: Confirm your drive type. Make sure scheduled optimization is enabled (for HDDs) and avoid manual defragmentation on SSDs. [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) automatically detects drive types and applies the appropriate optimization method.

Mistake 5: Ignoring Large Unnecessary Files and Duplicates

Disk Cleanup doesn’t always find large personal files or duplicates, which can take up significant space.

Solution: Use advanced tools like Glary Utilities’ Disk Analyzer and Duplicate File Finder to locate and safely remove these files.

How Do You Troubleshoot Disk Cleanup Issues?

Issue: Disk Cleanup Hangs or Fails

Sometimes, Disk Cleanup hangs during the “Windows Update Cleanup” or fails to finish.

Fix: Restart your computer, then run Disk Cleanup as an administrator. If the problem persists, use the built-in Windows Troubleshooter or try Glary Utilities’ 1-Click Maintenance to identify and resolve stubborn cleanup issues.

Issue: Not Enough Space Freed

If you run Disk Cleanup but see little improvement, temporary files or caches may be locked by active programs.

Fix: Close all open apps, restart, and run Disk Cleanup again. Use [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) to access advanced cleaning modules that can clean browser caches, application leftovers, and more.

Issue: Disk Optimization Is Unavailable

If Windows says that optimization can’t run, it may be due to drive errors or unsupported filesystems.

Fix: Open Command Prompt as administrator and run "chkdsk /f" on the affected drive. Once fixed, use Glary Utilities’ Disk Repair to scan for and fix deeper disk issues before attempting to optimize again.
