Section 1: Basic Optimization – Quick Wins for Beginners

What Are the First Steps to Speed Up My PC?

If you’re just starting out with PC maintenance, focus on simple, effective actions that bring noticeable improvements with minimal effort. Here are a few to get you started:

1\. Remove Unnecessary Startup Programs

Many programs automatically launch when Windows starts, slowing boot time. To manage these:

\- Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Click the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable unneeded programs.

2\. Clean Up Junk Files

Temporary files, browser caches, and other digital clutter can accumulate quickly, eating up valuable storage and affecting system performance.

\- Open Windows Settings > System > Storage and use Storage Sense for a quick cleanup. - Alternatively, use Glary Utilities’ 1-Click Maintenance tool. It scans for junk files, temporary files, and other common clutter, allowing you to clean out gigabytes of wasted space in seconds.

Updates often include performance improvements and bug fixes.

\- Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update to check for system updates. - For drivers, you can use Device Manager, or again rely on [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)’ Driver Manager to scan for outdated drivers and update them automatically.
