Windows 10 comes with many features designed to make your computer more connected and personalized. However, these features often collect data in the background. For beginners, configuring privacy settings properly is an important step to keep your personal information safe, reduce unwanted tracking, and maintain control of your system. Below are ten practical ways to enhance privacy in Windows 10 and ensure a more secure computing experience.

1\. How can you control diagnostic data collection? Windows 10 sends diagnostic data to Microsoft to help improve the system. You can reduce the amount of data collected. Go to Start > Settings > Privacy > Diagnostics & feedback. Set the option to “Required diagnostic data” instead of “Optional diagnostic data.” This minimizes what Microsoft collects about your device usage.

2\. What steps help manage app permissions? Apps installed from the Microsoft Store often request access to your camera, microphone, location, or contacts. To control this, open Settings > Privacy and choose categories like Camera or Microphone. Turn off access for apps that do not need it. For example, a weather app may need your location, but a calculator should not.

3\. How do you disable advertising ID tracking? Windows assigns your device an advertising ID to personalize ads. To stop this tracking, go to Settings > Privacy > General and switch off “Let apps use advertising ID.” Ads will still appear, but they won’t be based on your personal usage patterns.

4\. Can you limit location tracking? Location tracking is useful for maps but not always necessary. Open Settings > Privacy > Location. Turn off location access if you do not use it. Alternatively, you can allow location only for specific apps instead of all apps at once.

5\. How can you manage background apps? Apps running in the background can use data and access information without you noticing. Go to Settings > Privacy > Background apps. Turn off unnecessary apps from running in the background. This not only improves privacy but also reduces system resource usage.

6\. What about controlling Cortana’s data collection? Cortana, Microsoft’s digital assistant, gathers personal information to provide recommendations. If you do not use it, disable Cortana’s data collection by opening Settings > Cortana and turning off permissions such as location or contacts. This prevents unnecessary sharing of personal data.

7\. How do you manage Windows updates and peer-to-peer sharing? Windows Update includes a feature that shares update files with other PCs using your internet connection. To manage this, go to Settings > Update & Security > Delivery Optimization. Turn off “Allow downloads from other PCs.” This prevents your computer from sending update data to other users over the internet.

8\. Can you improve browser privacy in Microsoft Edge? Microsoft Edge includes privacy tools you can adjust. Open Edge, click the three dots in the top-right corner, then select Settings > Privacy, search, and services. Choose “Strict” under Tracking prevention to block most trackers from websites. This enhances privacy when browsing the internet.

9\. How do you clear activity history? Windows tracks your activity history across devices. To clear this, go to Settings > Privacy > Activity history. Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device” and “Send my activity history to Microsoft.” Click “Clear” under Clear activity history. This ensures past activities are not stored or synced.
