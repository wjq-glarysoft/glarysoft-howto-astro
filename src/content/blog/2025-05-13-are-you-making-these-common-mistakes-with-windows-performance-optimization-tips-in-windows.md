For many Windows users, keeping their computer running smoothly can feel like a daunting task. However, optimizing your PC’s performance doesn’t have to be complicated. Let's dive into some common mistakes beginners make and provide you with practical, actionable tips to improve your Windows system's performance.

What Are the Most Common Mistakes Beginners Make?

Mistake 1: Ignoring Software Updates Beginners often overlook regular updates, thinking they’re just optional. However, these updates contain essential security patches and performance improvements. Make sure Windows Update is enabled and regularly check for updates manually by going to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update.

Mistake 2: Letting Startup Programs Accumulate When too many programs start up with your computer, they can slow down your system. To manage this, press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. Then, navigate to the Startup tab. Here, you can disable programs you don’t need to start immediately by right-clicking on them and selecting Disable.

Mistake 3: Overlooking Disk Cleanup Windows accumulates temporary files that can eat up hard drive space. Use Disk Cleanup to remove unnecessary files. Access it by typing Disk Cleanup in the search bar and selecting the drive you want to clean. What Other Optimization Tips Should Beginners Consider?

1\. Adjust Visual Effects for Performance Windows visual effects can be resource-intensive. To adjust them, type "performance" in the search bar, and select Adjust the appearance and performance of Windows. Choose Adjust for best performance to disable unnecessary animations.

2\. Keep Your System Free of Malware Malware can severely impact performance. Ensure you have Windows Defender or a reliable third-party antivirus program running. Regularly scan your system to detect and eliminate any threats.

3\. Regularly Reboot Your System While it might seem trivial, rebooting your computer can clear out memory and close processes that are slowing down your system. Make it a habit to restart your computer every few days.
