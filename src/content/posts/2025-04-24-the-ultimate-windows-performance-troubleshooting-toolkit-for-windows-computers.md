What Are the Common Mistakes to Avoid When Troubleshooting Performance?

1\. Ignoring Regular Maintenance One of the most prevalent mistakes is neglecting regular system maintenance. Over time, your Windows PC accumulates unnecessary files, fragmented data, and unused applications that can slow down performance. It's essential to incorporate routine maintenance into your schedule.

2\. Overlooking Disk Cleanup Disk Cleanup is a built-in Windows tool that helps remove temporary files and system cache. However, many users overlook this simple yet effective tool. Running Disk Cleanup regularly can free up valuable hard drive space and improve system speed.

3\. Not Using a Comprehensive Cleaning Tool While Windows offers basic cleanup tools, they may not cover all aspects of system optimization. 3\. Manage Startup Programs Performance issues often arise from too many applications running at startup.
