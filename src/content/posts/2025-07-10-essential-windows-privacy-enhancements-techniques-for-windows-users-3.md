Protecting your privacy on Windows is more important than ever. From personal data to browsing habits, your computer holds valuable information that can be targeted by advertisers, hackers, or even the operating system itself. Fortunately, Windows offers a range of settings and tools to help you manage your privacy. In this step-by-step guide, you’ll learn easy, actionable ways to enhance your privacy on any Windows PC, from built-in settings to using trusted optimization tools like Glary Utilities.

Why Should You Enhance Your Privacy on Windows?

Windows is designed for usability and connectivity, but many features can share your data with Microsoft or third-party apps. By taking control of these settings, you can reduce unwanted tracking, minimize data exposure, and enjoy a more secure computing experience.

How Can You Review and Adjust Windows Privacy Settings?

1\. Open Windows Settings

Click the Start menu and select the Settings gear icon.

2\. Navigate to Privacy & Security

In the Settings window, click on Privacy & Security from the left sidebar.

3\. Review General Privacy Options

Under the Windows permissions section, select General. Here, disable options like:

\- Let apps use advertising ID for experiences across apps - Send Microsoft info about how I write - Show me suggested content in the Settings app

Turning these off reduces targeted advertising and data collection.

4\. Location Settings

Still under Privacy & Security, click on Location. You can:

\- Turn off location services entirely, or - Choose which apps can access your location by toggling each app's access

Turning off location services prevents Windows and apps from tracking your physical whereabouts.

5\. Camera and Microphone Permissions

Select Camera and Microphone from the sidebar. Disable access for apps that don’t require these functions. For example, keep access enabled for Zoom, but disable it for apps you don’t use for video calls.

6\. Diagnostic Data and Feedback

Scroll to Diagnostics & feedback. Select Send required diagnostic data only instead of Optional. This limits the amount of data sent to Microsoft. You can also turn off tailored experiences and delete diagnostic data.

How to Control App Permissions Effectively?

1\. Go to App Permissions

Within the Privacy & Security section, look for individual permissions like Contacts, Calendar, Call history, and Email.

2\. Review and Disable Unnecessary Access

Click on each category and turn off access for apps that don’t need this information. For instance, you may want to prevent games or unrelated apps from accessing your contacts or calendar.

What About Web and Search Privacy in Windows?

1\. Configure Search Permissions

Under Privacy & Security, select Search permissions.

\- Turn off Cloud content search unless you want Windows to search your content in OneDrive or Outlook. - Turn off History if you don’t want Windows to save your search history.

2\. Edge and Other Browsers

Open Microsoft Edge, go to Settings, then navigate to Privacy, search, and services.

\- Enable Tracking Prevention and set it to Strict. - Clear browsing data on exit for extra privacy.

If you use Chrome or Firefox, similar privacy options are available in their settings.

Can You Increase Privacy with a Standard User Account?

Running daily tasks with a standard user account (instead of an administrator account) can prevent unauthorized changes and reduce exposure to malware.

1\. Open Settings, go to Accounts, then Family & other users. 2. Create a new account and set it as Standard User. 3. Use this account for everyday computing, reserving the administrator account for installing software or making major changes.

Should You Update Windows Regularly?

Always keep your operating system and apps updated to patch privacy vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers.

\- Open Settings, go to Windows Update, and check for updates frequently.

What Are Additional Privacy Tips for All Windows Users?

\- Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication where possible. - Consider using a local account instead of a Microsoft account if you want to keep data more private. - Be cautious with third-party apps—only install software from trusted sources.
