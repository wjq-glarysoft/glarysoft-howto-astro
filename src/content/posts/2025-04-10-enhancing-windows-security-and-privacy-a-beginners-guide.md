As technology advances, the need for robust security and privacy measures becomes increasingly crucial. Windows users, particularly beginners, might feel overwhelmed by the variety of options available to secure their systems. This guide aims to provide practical, actionable advice on enhancing your Windows security and privacy, while introducing tools like Glary Utilities to simplify the process.

1\. Regular Updates

Keeping your system updated is the first step in securing your Windows PC. Windows Update ensures that your operating system is protected against the latest vulnerabilities. To check for updates:

\- Click on the Start menu and select Settings. - Go to Update & Security. - Click on Windows Update, then select Check for updates.

By enabling automatic updates, your system will regularly receive security patches and improvements. This minimizes the risk of exploitation through known vulnerabilities.

2\. Enable Windows Firewall

Windows Firewall acts as a barrier between your PC and potential threats from the internet. To ensure it's enabled:

\- Open the Control Panel and select System and Security. - Click on Windows Defender Firewall. - Select Turn Windows Defender Firewall on or off, and ensure it's set to On for both private and public networks.

3\. Use Antivirus Software

Installing reliable antivirus software is essential for detecting and removing malware. Windows Security, formerly known as Windows Defender, provides built-in protection. However, you may also consider third-party solutions for enhanced features.

4\. User Account Control (UAC)

UAC prevents unauthorized changes to your system by prompting for permission when a change is attempted. To adjust UAC settings:

\- Open the Control Panel and go to User Accounts. - Click on Change User Account Control settings. - Choose a security level that suits your needs. The default setting is recommended for most users.

5\. Secure Your Network

To protect your network:

\- Change the default login credentials for your router. - Use WPA3 encryption for Wi-Fi security. - Regularly update your router's firmware.

6\. Manage Privacy Settings

Windows 10 and 11 offer a range of privacy settings. Here's how you can customize them:

\- Open Settings and go to Privacy. - Review options under categories like General, Speech, and Location, and disable anything you're uncomfortable with.

8\. Backup Your Data

Regularly backing up your data protects against data loss from system failures, malware, or accidental deletions. You can use built-in tools like File History or third-party solutions to back up your important files and create system restore points.
