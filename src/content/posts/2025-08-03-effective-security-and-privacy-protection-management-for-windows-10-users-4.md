Protecting your personal data and keeping your Windows 10 PC secure can seem complicated, but with a few easy steps and the right habits, you can manage your security and privacy effectively. This guide will help beginner-level users apply the best practices for Windows 10 privacy and security using built-in features and trusted tools.

Why is Security and Privacy Important on Windows 10?

Your Windows 10 computer is often the main hub for your photos, documents, emails, and personal information. Without proper protection, your data could be exposed to viruses, hackers, or unwanted tracking. Good privacy and security habits not only keep your files safe but also maintain your PC’s performance and reliability.

How to Keep Windows 10 Updated?

One of the simplest yet most effective security steps is keeping Windows 10 up to date. Updates often include critical security patches that fix vulnerabilities.

1\. Click the Start menu and select Settings. 2. Go to Update & Security. 3. Click Windows Update, then select Check for updates. 4. Install all available updates and restart your PC if needed.

Allow updates to install automatically whenever possible. This ensures you’re always protected against new threats.

Which Built-in Security Tools Should You Use?

Windows 10 includes several built-in security tools that help defend your PC:

Windows Security (also called Windows Defender): This provides real-time antivirus and anti-malware protection. Make sure it’s enabled by going to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Security. Open Windows Security and check for any action needed.

Firewall: A firewall helps block suspicious traffic. In Windows Security, click Firewall & network protection. Ensure your firewall is on for all network types.

How Can You Manage App Permissions and Privacy Settings?

Windows 10 apps may request access to your location, microphone, camera, calendar, and more. You can control what data apps can access:

1\. Open Settings and click Privacy. 2. Review each section on the left (Location, Camera, Microphone, etc.). 3. Turn off access for apps you don’t trust or don’t use.

For example, if you don’t use your microphone, you can disable it for all apps. This minimizes the risk of accidental data leaks.

What Password Practices Should You Follow?

Strong, unique passwords for your accounts and your PC are vital. Here are some tips:

\- Use a password or PIN to sign into your computer. Go to Settings > Accounts > Sign-in options to set these up. - Choose passwords at least 12 characters long, mixing letters, numbers, and symbols. - Do not reuse passwords across different websites and accounts. - Consider using a password manager to securely store and manage your passwords.

How Do You Recognize and Avoid Phishing and Suspicious Links?

Phishing is a common way attackers steal your information. Learn to spot suspicious emails or websites:

\- Don’t click on links or open attachments from unknown senders. - Look at the sender’s email address carefully – a slight misspelling can be a sign of a fake. - When in doubt, access a website directly through your browser rather than clicking a link. - Use Microsoft Edge’s built-in security features, which can help block malicious websites.

How Do You Back Up Important Data?

A backup protects you if your data is lost or your PC is compromised.

\- Use File History: Go to Settings > Update & Security > Backup and set up File History to an external drive. - Consider cloud storage for important files (OneDrive is built into Windows 10).

Backing up regularly ensures you won’t lose important documents and photos.

What Should You Do If Your Computer is Compromised?

If you suspect a virus or unauthorized access:

\- Disconnect from the internet immediately. - Run a full scan with Windows Security. - Use Glary Utilities’ Malware Remover to help identify and remove threats. - Change your passwords from another device if needed.

If your PC behaves strangely after following these steps or you see signs of identity theft, contact a tech professional for assistance.
