Protecting your privacy on Windows 10 and 11 is more important than ever. From system telemetry to app permissions, Windows often collects and shares information that many users would prefer to keep private. Whether you’re a beginner or an advanced user, this guide provides practical, easy-to-follow steps to improve your privacy and security on your PC.

Beginner Level: Essential Windows Privacy Settings

What Are the Key Privacy Settings Every User Should Check?

Windows makes it easy to adjust key privacy settings, but many are enabled by default. Here’s how to review and adjust them:

Step 1: Open the Privacy Settings Click the Start menu and select Settings (the gear icon). Choose Privacy & security from the left sidebar.

Step 2: Adjust General Privacy Options Under the General tab, disable options like: - Let apps use advertising ID - Send Microsoft info about how I write - Show me suggested content in the Settings app

Step 3: Limit Location Access Go to Location and turn off location access for the device or restrict which apps can use your location.

Step 4: Review Diagnostic Data In Diagnostics & feedback, set the option to Required diagnostic data only.

Step 5: Control App Permissions Scroll through permissions like Camera, Microphone, Contacts, and Calendar. Toggle off access for apps that don’t need it.

Intermediate Level: Strengthen Your Digital Footprint

How Can You Minimize Tracking and Data Sharing?

Step 1: Disable Cortana and Web Search Open Settings > Privacy & security > Search permissions. Turn off Cloud content search and History.

Step 2: Manage Activity History In Privacy & security, choose Activity history. Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” Click “Clear” to remove existing activity history.

Step 3: Check Microsoft Account Privacy Dashboard Visit https://account.microsoft.com/privacy. Review and delete data such as browsing history, location activity, and Cortana voice recordings.

Step 4: Harden Your Web Browsing Switch to privacy-focused browsers like Firefox or Brave. Use browser extensions like uBlock Origin and HTTPS Everywhere.

Advanced Level: Take Full Control of Your Data

What Advanced Steps Can You Take to Enhance Privacy?

Step 1: Modify Group Policy Settings (Windows Pro or Enterprise) Press Windows + R, type gpedit.msc, and press Enter. Navigate to Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > Windows Components > Data Collection and Preview Builds. Double-click “Allow Telemetry” and set it to Disabled.

Step 2: Debloat Windows Use PowerShell scripts or specialized tools to remove unwanted apps and telemetry services. For example: Open PowerShell as Administrator Type: Get-AppxPackage \*xbox\* | Remove-AppxPackage Research reputable debloating scripts tailored to your needs.

Step 3: Harden Network Privacy Disable Wi-Fi Sense in Network & Internet settings. Set your network to Private instead of Public.

Step 4: Encrypt Your Data Turn on BitLocker (Windows Pro) from Control Panel > System and Security > BitLocker Drive Encryption.
