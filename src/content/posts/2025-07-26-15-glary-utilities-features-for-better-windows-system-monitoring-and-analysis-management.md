Why Monitor and Analyze Your Windows System?

A well-monitored system is less prone to slowdowns, instability, or security threats. By regularly analyzing your Windows environment, you can spot anomalies, identify potential issues before they become serious, and make informed decisions about maintenance and upgrades.

1\. Process Manager: What’s Eating Your Resources? Glary Utilities’ Process Manager goes beyond the standard Task Manager. It allows you to view all running processes, see their CPU and memory usage, and quickly end any unwanted or suspicious tasks. If your PC suddenly slows down, use this tool to instantly pinpoint the culprit—such as a rogue browser tab or background app.

2\. Disk Space Analyzer: Where’s Your Hard Drive Space Going? Low storage can cripple performance. The Disk Space Analyzer visualizes which files and folders are taking up the most space. For example, after months of downloads or video editing, you can use this feature to locate and remove large, obsolete files or duplicates.

3\. Startup Manager: Are You Slowing Down Boot Times? Many programs add themselves to Windows startup, increasing boot times. The Startup Manager lists every startup item, its impact, and lets you enable, disable, or delay them. For instance, you can defer non-essential apps like cloud sync clients to speed up your system’s launch.

4\. Registry Cleaner and Analyzer: Is Your Registry Bloated? A cluttered registry can lead to errors and slow performance. Glary Utilities not only cleans but also analyzes registry health, highlighting problematic entries. Advanced users can review individual keys before cleaning, while beginners can use the one-click option.

5\. System Information: Do You Know Your Hardware and Software Specs? Quickly view detailed summaries of your CPU, RAM, motherboard, and installed software. This is invaluable when troubleshooting compatibility issues or planning hardware upgrades. For example, check your RAM amount before deciding to run memory-intensive applications.

6\. Duplicate Files Finder: Are Unneeded Copies Wasting Space? Duplicate files can accumulate from backups or downloads. This feature scans your drives and presents identical files for review.
