Why Does Windows 11 Need Optimization?

Windows 11 introduces a sleek interface and improved features, but over time, even the fastest systems can slow down due to background processes, unnecessary files, or outdated settings. Optimizing your PC ensures faster startups, smoother performance, and a better overall experience.

What Are the First Steps for Quick Performance Gains?

Clean Up Temporary Files and Junk

Temporary files, cache, and leftover data accumulate as you use your PC, consuming valuable storage and slowing down your system.

Manual Cleanup: 1. Press Windows + R, type %temp%, and press Enter. 2. Select all files and delete them. 3. Empty the Recycle Bin.

Disable Unnecessary Startup Programs

Programs that launch at startup can significantly slow down boot times.

How to manage startup items: 1. Right-click the Taskbar and select Task Manager. 2. Go to the Startup tab. 3. Disable programs you don’t need at boot. Right-click and select Disable.

How Can You Reduce Background Resource Usage?

Manage Background Apps

Many apps run in the background, consuming CPU, memory, and battery.

To control background apps manually: 1. Go to Settings > Apps > Installed apps. 2. Click on the three dots next to an app and choose Advanced options. 3. Under Background apps permissions, select Never.

With Glary Utilities, use the Process Manager to review running processes and pinpoint resource hogs. You can terminate unnecessary background tasks directly from the tool.

Should You Update Drivers and Windows Regularly?

Drivers and system updates ensure compatibility and improved performance.

Update Windows: 1. Open Settings > Windows Update. 2. Click Check for updates and install available updates.

Update drivers: 1. Right-click Start, select Device Manager. 2. Right-click devices (such as Display adapters) and choose Update driver.

What About Disk Optimization and Fragmentation?

While SSDs do not need traditional defragmentation, HDD users can benefit from occasional defrags to improve read/write speeds.

Manual Disk Optimization: 1. Search for Defragment and Optimize Drives in the Start menu. 2. Select your drive and click Optimize.

How Do You Optimize System Visual Effects for Speed?

Windows 11 visual effects look great but can strain older PCs.

To adjust for performance: 1. Right-click Start, select System. 2. Click Advanced system settings > Performance > Settings. 3. Choose Adjust for best performance or manually deselect unnecessary effects.
