Windows users often overlook the importance of maintaining robust privacy and security on their PCs. With threats constantly evolving, relying on built-in tools is not always enough. Why Focus on Privacy and Security?

Every action on your computer—browsing, installing apps, or even deleting files—can leave behind data that may be vulnerable to unauthorized access. Optimizing privacy and security isn’t just about antivirus software; it’s about controlling your digital footprint and ensuring your information stays protected. Glary Utilities provides a suite of tools specifically designed to address these challenges.

1\. Secure File Shredder

Beginners: Why Use File Shredder? When you delete files normally in Windows, they often remain recoverable. This means sensitive data like financial records or personal documents could be retrieved by someone else.

How to Use: 1. Open Glary Utilities. 2. Select “File Shredder” from the Advanced Tools menu. 3. Add files or folders you want to permanently erase. 4. Click “Shred Now.”

Advanced: Customizing Shredding Methods Advanced users can choose multiple-pass algorithms for shredding, making data recovery nearly impossible. This is crucial for confidential business files or when disposing of old drives.

2\. Privacy Cleaner

Beginners: What Does Privacy Cleaner Do? It removes browsing histories, cache, cookies, and usage traces from Windows and popular browsers. This helps prevent tracking and unauthorized snooping.

How to Use: 1. Launch Glary Utilities. 2. Go to “1-Click Maintenance.” 3. Check the Privacy Cleaner option. 4. Click “Scan,” then “Repair Problems.”

Advanced: Scheduling Automatic Cleaning Set up scheduled cleans for regular privacy protection. Go to “Schedule Tasks” and customize when [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) should run Privacy Cleaner automatically, ensuring your tracks are regularly erased.

3\. Tracks Eraser

Beginners: Why Erase Tracks? Windows and applications log your activities—like recently opened files and search queries. Tracks Eraser wipes these logs so others can’t reconstruct what you’ve done.

How to Use: 1. In Glary Utilities, open “Tracks Eraser.” 2. Review the items to erase (recent docs, clipboard, etc.). 3. Click “Erase Now.”

Advanced: Selective Erasure Advanced users can customize exactly which tracks to erase, helping balance privacy needs with usability. For example, you might choose to keep recent Office documents for productivity, but erase browsing traces.

4\. Startup Manager (Security Focus)

Beginners: Why Manage Startup Programs? Some software starts automatically with Windows, including potentially unwanted or malicious programs. Managing these improves security and speeds up boot time.

How to Use: 1. Open Glary Utilities. 2. Go to “Startup Manager.” 3. Review and disable unnecessary or suspicious entries.

Advanced: Assessing Startup Risk For advanced users, Glary Utilities provides details on each startup item. Use this information to research unknown programs and prevent malware or spyware from launching at startup.

5\. Disk Cleaner (Sensitive Data)

Beginners: Cleaning Up Sensitive Data Temporary files, crash dumps, and leftover installation files can contain sensitive information. Disk Cleaner removes these safely.

How to Use: 1. Access “Disk Cleaner” in Glary Utilities. 2. Select drives to scan. 3. Review results and choose what to delete.

Advanced: Deep-Cleaning Customization Configure Disk Cleaner to target specific file types or locations. For example, advanced users can set it to clean old system restore points that may contain outdated but sensitive information.

6\. Context Menu Manager

Beginners: Understanding Context Menu Risks Malware often adds entries to your right-click context menu, providing backdoor access or tracking your actions.

How to Use: 1. Open “Context Menu Manager” in Glary Utilities. 2. Identify and disable unknown or unnecessary context menu items.

Advanced: Manual Entry Management Advanced users can review file associations and registry entries for deeper control. Use this feature to spot and remove entries left behind by uninstalled apps that may still pose a security risk.

7\. Software Update Tool

Beginners: Why Keep Software Updated? Outdated software can have vulnerabilities that hackers exploit. Glary Utilities’ Software Update tool checks for the latest versions of installed programs.

How to Use: 1. Select “Software Update” in Glary Utilities. 2. Scan for outdated apps. 3. Follow prompts to update them.

Advanced: Patch Management Strategy For advanced users, set up reminders for critical updates or automate the update process for high-risk programs. This ensures your system is always protected against known threats.
