Task scheduling allows Windows to perform regular system maintenance, backups, updates, and custom tasks automatically. If not set up correctly, scheduled tasks may fail, overlap, or even compromise your PC’s performance and security.

What Common Mistakes Do Beginners Make with Task Scheduling?

Beginners often: - Overload their schedules with unnecessary tasks - Forget to review or update scheduled tasks - Ignore tasks that can slow down the system at busy times - Miss critical system maintenance due to poor scheduling - Fail to monitor failed or outdated tasks
