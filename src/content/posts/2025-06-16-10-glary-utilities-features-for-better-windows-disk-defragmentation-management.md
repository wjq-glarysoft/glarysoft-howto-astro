Why Does Disk Defragmentation Matter in Windows?

Over time, files on your HDD can become fragmented—split into pieces and scattered across the drive. This slows down file access and system performance. Defragmentation reorganizes these fragments, leading to faster read/write speeds and a healthier PC. Common Questions and Tips

Is Disk Defragmentation Necessary on SSDs?

No. SSDs don’t benefit from defragmentation and can be harmed by frequent writes. Glary Utilities detects SSDs and disables defragmentation options for these drives, ensuring hardware longevity.

Can I Use My PC During Defragmentation?

Yes, but for best results, schedule defragmentation when you’re not actively using the PC to avoid performance drops and file access conflicts.

How Often Should I Defragment?

For regular home use, monthly scheduling is sufficient. Heavier users (e.g., video editing) may benefit from weekly routines.
