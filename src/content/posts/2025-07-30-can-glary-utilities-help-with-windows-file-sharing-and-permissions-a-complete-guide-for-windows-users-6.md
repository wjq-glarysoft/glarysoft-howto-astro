What Are Windows File Sharing and Permissions?

In Windows, file sharing lets you give other users access to your files or folders. This is useful at home (sharing photos, documents, or music with family members) or at work (collaborating on projects). Permissions are rules you set to decide who can view, modify, or delete your files.

For example, you might want your family to see your holiday photos on the network, but not to delete them. Or you may need to share work documents with colleagues, but only allow them to read, not edit.

How Do You Share Files and Folders in Windows?

Windows makes it easy to share files and folders on your PC. Here’s a simple example:

1\. Open File Explorer and find the folder you want to share. 2. Right-click the folder and select “Give access to” or “Properties.” 3. Go to the “Sharing” tab. 4. Click “Share.” 5. Select the people you want to share with, or choose “Everyone” for all users on your network. 6. Choose their permission level: "Read" (can only view) or "Read/Write" (can view and edit). 7. Click “Share” and then “Done.”

That’s it! Your folder is now shared with the chosen people.

How Do Permissions Work in Windows?

Permissions control what users can do with shared files. The main types are:

Read: User can view files but not change them. Write: User can change or add files. Full Control: User can do anything, including deleting files.

To change permissions:

1\. Right-click the folder, select “Properties,” and go to the “Security” tab. 2. Click “Edit” to change permissions for users or groups. 3. Select a user, then check the permissions you want to give. 4. Click “Apply” and “OK.”
