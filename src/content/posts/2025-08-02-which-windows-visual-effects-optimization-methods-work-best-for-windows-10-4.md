Windows 10 comes packed with visual effects such as animations, shadows, and smooth transitions. While these features make your computer look modern and attractive, they can sometimes slow down your system—especially if you’re using an older or budget device. Many beginners attempt to speed up their PC by tweaking these settings, but are unsure which changes actually improve performance.

What Are Common Mistakes When Optimizing Windows Visual Effects?

A frequent beginner mistake is to turn off all visual effects without understanding their function. While this may give your PC a slight speed boost, it can make Windows look outdated and harder to use. Another error is tweaking individual settings randomly, which may have little impact or even make certain features less accessible. Overlooking reliable optimization tools, like Glary Utilities, is another missed opportunity for easy, safe improvements.

Which Visual Effects Should You Adjust for Best Performance?

Windows 10 provides several visual effects options. Here’s how to make smart choices:

1\. Access the Correct Settings: - Right-click on the Start button and choose System. - Click “Advanced system settings” on the left. - Under the Performance section, click Settings.

2\. Understand the Options: - Let Windows choose: Windows decides based on your hardware. - Adjust for best appearance: Turns all effects on. - Adjust for best performance: Turns all effects off. - Custom: Allows you to select which effects to keep or remove.

If your PC feels slow, choose “Adjust for best performance” and then enable a few effects that help usability: - Show thumbnails instead of icons (so you can preview images and videos easily). - Smooth edges of screen fonts (for clearer text).

Avoid disabling all effects—especially those related to font smoothing and thumbnail previews—as they provide genuine benefits.
