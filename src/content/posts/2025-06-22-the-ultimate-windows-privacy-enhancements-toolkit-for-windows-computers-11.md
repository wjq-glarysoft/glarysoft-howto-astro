Privacy on Windows computers is a growing concern for many users. Windows 10 and 11, while powerful and user-friendly, come with numerous settings and features that can share your personal information with Microsoft and third parties. Protecting your privacy requires a proactive approach, but you don’t have to be an expert to take control. This toolkit is designed to provide intermediate Windows users with practical, step-by-step enhancements to safeguard privacy while maintaining your system’s performance and usability.

Why Does Windows Collect Personal Data?

Windows collects data for several reasons, including improving user experience, troubleshooting issues, and targeted advertising. Telemetry, diagnostic data, advertising IDs, and location services are just a few ways your information can be tracked or shared. Understanding these methods is the first step in regaining control over your privacy.

How Can You Adjust Privacy Settings in Windows?

Windows provides several built-in options to manage privacy. Here’s how to access and adjust the most important ones:

1\. Open the Settings app by pressing the Windows key + I. 2. Go to Privacy & security. 3. Review the sections on the left panel, such as General, Speech, Inking & typing personalization, Diagnostics & feedback, and Activity history.

For each section, turn off options you’re uncomfortable with. For example: - Under General, disable options like “Let apps use advertising ID” and “Send Microsoft info about how I write.” - In Diagnostics & feedback, select “Required diagnostic data” only and turn off “Tailored experiences.”

Should You Limit Location and Camera Access?

Many apps request access to your location, camera, and microphone. Control these permissions to minimize unnecessary sharing:

1\. In the Privacy & security menu, select Location, Camera, and Microphone one by one. 2. Toggle off access for apps that don’t require these features. 3. Consider turning off location services completely if you rarely use location-based apps.

How Can You Manage App Permissions?

Apps installed from the Microsoft Store and third-party sources often request permissions during setup.

1\. Review the App permissions section in Privacy & security. 2. Disable access to sensitive data such as Contacts, Calendar, and Call history for apps that do not need this information.

Is Your Activity History Being Shared?

The Activity History feature lets Windows collect and sync your activities across devices. To turn this off:

1\. Go to Privacy & security > Activity history. 2. Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” 3. Uncheck “Send my activity history to Microsoft.”

How Do You Remove Cortana and Online Speech Recognition?

Cortana, Microsoft’s voice assistant, requires sending voice data to the cloud for processing.

To disable online speech recognition and Cortana: 1. Go to Privacy & security > Speech. 2. Switch off “Online speech recognition.” 3. If Cortana is running, right-click on the Cortana icon in the taskbar and select “Hide from taskbar.”

What Steps Should You Take to Secure Your Web Browsing?

Web browsers can leak personal data through cookies, trackers, and saved passwords. Improve your security with these actions: - Use private browsing modes (InPrivate for Edge, Incognito for Chrome). - Regularly clear your browsing data. - Install privacy-focused browser extensions such as uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger. - Set your browser to “Do Not Track” mode.

What Are Some Additional Tips for Enhanced Privacy?

\- Use a local account instead of a Microsoft account, if you don’t need cloud syncing. - Keep Windows updated to ensure you have the latest privacy protections. - Enable BitLocker or another drive encryption method to protect your data if your device is lost or stolen. - Disable Nearby Sharing and Bluetooth when not in use.
