Keeping your Windows 10 or 11 PC running smoothly isn’t complicated, but it does require a bit of regular maintenance. For beginners, understanding which maintenance tasks really make a difference—and which tools to use—can prevent headaches, slowdowns, and errors before they happen. This guide will walk you through the essential maintenance steps, introduce you to the built-in system tools, and recommend a user-friendly solution to make the process even easier.

Why is Regular Windows Maintenance Important?

Like any machine, your PC benefits from periodic upkeep. Over time, clutter builds up on your hard drive, apps become outdated, and small issues can snowball into major problems. Regular maintenance helps you:

\- Improve system speed - Free up valuable disk space - Reduce errors and crashes - Extend the lifespan of your hardware

What Are the Essential Windows Maintenance Tasks?

1\. Disk Cleanup: Removing Unnecessary Files

Temporary files, system cache, and leftover data from updates can hog storage space and slow down your PC. Windows comes with a built-in tool called “Disk Cleanup” that helps clear this clutter.

How to use Disk Cleanup: 1. Press the Windows key and type “Disk Cleanup.” 2. Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:). 3. Check the boxes for file types you want to remove (like Temporary files). 4. Click “OK”, then “Delete Files” to confirm.

2\. Uninstall Unused Programs

Old or unused software can take up space and sometimes run background processes that slow down your PC.

How to uninstall programs: 1. Open Settings (Windows key + I). 2. Go to “Apps” > “Apps & features.” 3. Find programs you no longer use, click them, and select “Uninstall.”

3\. Manage Startup Programs

Too many startup apps can slow down boot times. It’s a good idea to review what launches when your PC turns on.

How to manage startup apps: 1. Right-click on the taskbar and select “Task Manager.” 2. Go to the “Startup” tab. 3. Right-click and disable any unnecessary apps.

Glary Utilities includes a “Startup Manager” that gives even clearer insight into which programs affect startup speed, with easy toggles to enable or disable them.

4\. Update Windows and Device Drivers

Keeping Windows and your device drivers up to date is crucial for performance and security.

How to update Windows: 1. Open Settings. 2. Go to “Update & Security” > “Windows Update.” 3. Click “Check for updates.”

For drivers, Windows Update often covers the basics. For more advanced driver management, Glary Utilities offers a driver updater tool that can automatically detect outdated drivers and update them for you.

5\. Run a Malware Scan

Malware can cause performance issues and put your data at risk. Windows Defender, built into Windows 10 and 11, is a straightforward, effective solution.

How to run a scan: 1. Type “Windows Security” in the search bar. 2. Open “Virus & threat protection.” 3. Click “Quick scan.”

6\. Defragment Your Hard Drive (HDD only)

If your PC uses a traditional hard drive (HDD), defragmenting can help keep files organized and improve speed.

How to defragment: 1. Type “Defragment and Optimize Drives” in the search bar. 2. Select your drive and click “Optimize.”

For SSDs, defragmenting isn’t needed—Windows will automatically optimize them in the background.
