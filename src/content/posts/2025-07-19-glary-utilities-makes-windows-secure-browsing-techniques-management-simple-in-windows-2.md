Why is Secure Browsing Important on Windows?

The internet is filled with risks such as phishing sites, malware downloads, and persistent trackers that monitor your online activity. Windows, being the most popular operating system, is often targeted by cybercriminals. Implementing secure browsing techniques helps protect your identity, financial data, browsing history, and even your PC’s performance.

Beginner-Friendly Secure Browsing Tips

1\. Keep Windows Updated

Regular updates patch vulnerabilities that attackers can exploit. Always enable Windows Update to automatically download critical security patches and browser upgrades.

2\. Use Trusted Browsers

Stick to reputable browsers like Microsoft Edge, Google Chrome, or Mozilla Firefox. These browsers offer built-in security features such as sandboxing and phishing protection.

3\. Clear Browsing Data Regularly

Over time, browsers accumulate cookies, cache, and history that could be exploited or compromise your privacy. Glary Utilities simplifies this with its 'Tracks Eraser' tool.

4\. Use Private Browsing Modes

Browsers offer “incognito” or “private” modes that don’t save browsing history, cookies, or temporary files. This is helpful for quick, private sessions.

Intermediate to Advanced Secure Browsing Practices

1\. Manage Browser Extensions and Plugins

Extensions can enhance browsing but also introduce vulnerabilities. Periodically review installed add-ons and remove ones you no longer trust or need.

2\. Set up DNS over HTTPS (DoH)

DNS over HTTPS encrypts your domain name lookups, making it harder for outsiders to see which sites you visit. Modern browsers and Windows 11 support DoH natively.

3\. Use a Robust Password Manager

Avoid storing passwords in browsers. Instead, use a dedicated password manager for stronger, unique passwords.

4\. Block Trackers and Third-Party Cookies

Advanced browsers and some privacy-oriented extensions can block third-party cookies and trackers. Regularly reviewing these settings reduces your exposure to online profiling.
