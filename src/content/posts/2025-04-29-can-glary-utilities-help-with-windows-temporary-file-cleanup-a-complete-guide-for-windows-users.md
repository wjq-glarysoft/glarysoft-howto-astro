What Are Windows Temporary Files?

Before diving into cleanup solutions, it's essential to understand what Windows temporary files are. These are files created by Windows and various applications to temporarily hold information. They are typically used for backup purposes, data transfer, or caching. Over time, these files accumulate and can take up significant space on your hard drive.

Why Clean Up Temporary Files?

Temporary files can bloat your system, slowing down performance and reducing available disk space. Regular cleanup is crucial to ensure your system runs smoothly and efficiently. When left unchecked, the buildup of temporary files can cause slow boot times, application crashes, and other performance issues.
