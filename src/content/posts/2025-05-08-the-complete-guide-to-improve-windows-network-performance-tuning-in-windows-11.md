As an intermediate Windows user, you’re likely looking for ways to enhance your network performance on Windows 11. Efficient network tuning can significantly improve your online experience, whether it’s faster browsing, seamless streaming, or smoother online gaming. In this guide, we’ll explore various strategies to optimize your network settings and enhance performance.

What Are the Basic Network Settings to Check?

Before diving into advanced configurations, ensure the basics are in place:

1\. Check Network Connection: Ensure your Ethernet cable is securely connected or that your Wi-Fi signal is strong and stable. 2. Restart Your Router: Sometimes, simply rebooting your router can resolve connection issues. Unplug it, wait for 10 seconds, and plug it back in. 3. Update Network Drivers: Navigate to Device Manager, expand Network Adapters, right-click your adapter, and select Update Driver to ensure you have the latest drivers.

How Can You Optimize with Windows Settings?

Windows 11 offers several built-in tools and settings you can tweak for better performance:

1\. Change DNS Server: - Go to Settings > Network & Internet > Status. - Click on Properties next to your network connection. - Under IP settings, select Edit, then choose Manual. - Enable IPv4 and enter a public DNS like Google DNS (8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4) or Cloudflare (1.1.1.1).

2\. Disable Background Apps: - Open Settings > Apps > Apps & features. - Click on an app you don't need to run in the background and select Advanced options. - Under the Background apps permissions, select Never.

3\. Configure Bandwidth Settings for Windows Update: - Open Settings > Windows Update > Advanced options > Delivery Optimization. - Click Advanced options and set the percentage limit for how much bandwidth is used for downloading updates.

How Can You Use Advanced Network Configuration?

For those ready to delve deeper, these advanced settings can further refine your network performance:

1\. Enable QoS (Quality of Service): - Access your router’s settings through a web browser (usually via 192.168.1.1). - Look for a QoS setting and prioritize traffic for specific applications or devices.

2\. Adjust Power Settings: - Go to Control Panel > Power Options. - Select Change plan settings next to your chosen plan, then Change advanced power settings. - Expand Wireless Adapter Settings and select Maximum Performance for both On battery and Plugged in.

3\. Use TCP Optimizer: - Download and run TCP Optimizer, a free tool designed to optimize your TCP/IP settings. - Choose your network adapter and set the connection speed, then apply the recommended settings.
