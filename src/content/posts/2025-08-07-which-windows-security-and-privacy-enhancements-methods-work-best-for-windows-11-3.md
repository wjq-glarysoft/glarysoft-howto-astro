Keeping your Windows 11 PC secure and your personal information private is more important than ever. While Windows 11 offers many built-in tools for security and privacy, knowing how to use them and supplementing with additional steps can make a big difference. This guide provides practical, step-by-step advice for both beginners and advanced users who want to maximize their Windows 11 privacy and security.

Why is security and privacy important on Windows 11?

Every day, your PC faces threats such as malware, phishing attacks, and unwanted data collection. Privacy risks also increase with apps and services that collect or share your information. Enhancing your security and privacy settings reduces these risks and helps keep your data safe.

What are the most effective Windows 11 security and privacy methods for beginners?

Enable Windows Security

Windows Security is the built-in antivirus and threat protection tool in Windows 11.

Step-by-step instructions: 1. Click Start and select Settings. 2. Go to Privacy & security > Windows Security. 3. Open Virus & threat protection and ensure real-time protection is turned on.

Turn on Windows Firewall

A firewall is a barrier that blocks unauthorized access to your computer.

Steps to check: 1. In Settings, go to Privacy & security > Windows Security > Firewall & network protection. 2. Make sure the firewall is on for Domain, Private, and Public networks.

Keep Windows Updated

Updates often include important security patches.

How to check for updates: 1. Go to Settings > Windows Update. 2. Click Check for updates and install any available updates.

Adjust Privacy Settings

Windows 11 collects diagnostic and usage data by default. Adjust these settings for better privacy.

How to adjust: 1. Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback. 2. Set Diagnostic data to Required only. 3. Turn off options like Tailored experiences and View diagnostic data.

Manage App Permissions

Restricting app access to features like location or microphone can protect your privacy.

Steps: 1. Go to Settings > Privacy & security. 2. Select the feature (e.g., Location, Microphone, Camera). 3. Toggle off permissions for apps that do not need access.

What advanced privacy and security enhancements are recommended for experienced users?

Use BitLocker to Encrypt Your Drive

BitLocker encryption protects your data even if your device is lost or stolen.

How to enable: 1. Search for "Manage BitLocker" in the Start menu. 2. Select Turn on BitLocker and follow the prompts to encrypt your drive.

Set Up Controlled Folder Access

This feature helps protect files from ransomware.

Steps: 1. Go to Windows Security > Virus & threat protection. 2. Under Ransomware protection, select Manage ransomware protection. 3. Turn on Controlled folder access and add folders you want to protect.

Check for Unnecessary Services and Startup Programs

Disabling unneeded background services and startup programs reduces your attack surface.

Instructions: 1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Go to the Startup tab and disable programs you don’t need at startup. 3. In Glary Utilities, use the "Startup Manager" to review and manage startup items more thoroughly.

Enable Secure Sign-In Options

Using Windows Hello (facial recognition, fingerprint, or PIN) adds an extra layer of sign-in security.

How to set up: 1. Go to Settings > Accounts > Sign-in options. 2. Choose and configure your preferred sign-in method.

Review and Harden Network Settings

Advanced users can limit remote access and disable unused network protocols.

Suggestions: - Disable Remote Desktop if not needed (Settings > System > Remote Desktop). - Turn off file and printer sharing if not in use (Control Panel > Network and Sharing Center > Advanced sharing settings).

How can you maintain security and privacy over time?

Regular Maintenance

Schedule regular scans with Windows Security and cleanups with Glary Utilities.

Update Third-Party Programs

Outdated software can have unpatched vulnerabilities. Use [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)’ "Software Update" feature to check for program updates.

Educate Yourself About Phishing

Being aware of common phishing tactics helps you avoid falling victim to scams. Do not open suspicious emails or links, and always verify requests for personal information.

Summary

By following these steps, you can keep your Windows 11 device secure and your personal information private.
