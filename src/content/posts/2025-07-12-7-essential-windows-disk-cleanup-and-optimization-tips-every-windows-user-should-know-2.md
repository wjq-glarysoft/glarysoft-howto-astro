Why Clean Up and Optimize Your Windows Disk?

Over time, your computer accumulates unnecessary files and programs that can slow it down or use up precious space. Regular disk cleanup and optimization helps your PC start faster, run smoother, and last longer.

What Temporary Files Can You Safely Delete?

Windows and many of its programs create temporary files that are useful only for short periods. These include internet cache, error logs, and leftover files from software updates. Removing them can free up considerable space.

How to delete temporary files: 1. Press Windows + R, type temp, and press Enter. Delete all visible files. 2. Repeat with %temp% to clear user-specific temporary files. 3. Open Settings > System > Storage > Temporary files. Check the boxes for files to remove and click Remove files.

Which Programs Can You Uninstall to Free Up Space?

Unused or rarely used programs take up space and can slow your system. Review your installed applications and remove the ones you don’t need.

How to uninstall programs: 1. Open Control Panel > Programs > Programs and Features. 2. Browse the list, select the program you want to remove, and click Uninstall.

How Does Disk Cleanup Improve Performance?

The built-in Disk Cleanup tool finds and deletes unnecessary files, such as system error memory dumps and old Windows installations.

How to use Disk Cleanup: 1. Search for ‘Disk Cleanup’ in the Start menu. 2. Select your system drive, usually C:, and click OK. 3. Check the boxes for the types of files you wish to remove, then click OK and confirm deletion.

Why Should You Defragment Your Hard Drive?

Files become fragmented and scattered on your hard drive over time, slowing down read/write speeds. Defragmenting reorganizes the data for faster access.

How to defragment your drive: 1. Type 'defragment' in the Start menu and open 'Defragment and Optimize Drives.' 2. Select your hard drive and click Optimize.

Note: You should not defragment SSDs—Windows will automatically optimize them. Glary Utilities detects your drive type and recommends the correct optimization method.

How Can You Manage Startup Programs for a Faster Boot?

Too many programs starting with Windows can slow down your computer’s boot time. Restrict startup apps to only those you really need.

How to manage startup programs: 1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Click the Startup tab. 3. Right-click unnecessary startup items and choose Disable.
