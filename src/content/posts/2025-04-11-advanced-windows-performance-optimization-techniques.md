Optimizing Windows performance isn't just about cleaning up temporary files or defragmenting your hard drive—though those can help. For users seeking more advanced techniques to get the most out of their system, deeper tweaks and adjustments will be necessary. Here are some advanced strategies to optimize your Windows PC performance effectively.

1\. Adjust Windows for Best Performance

Windows allows users to choose between performance and appearance. For those who prioritize speed over visual effects, adjusting Windows settings to prioritize performance can be a significant boost.

To do this: - Right-click on 'This PC' and select 'Properties'. - Click on 'Advanced system settings' on the left pane. - Under the 'Performance' section, click 'Settings'. - Select 'Adjust for best performance'. - Click 'Apply', then 'OK'.

This adjustment disables most of the visual effects that can slow down your system.

2\. Manage Startup Programs

Many programs are set to launch at startup, and this can significantly affect your boot time and overall system performance. Using Windows Task Manager, you can disable unnecessary startup programs.

Steps: - Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Navigate to the 'Startup' tab. - Review the list of programs and disable those not needed at startup by right-clicking on them and selecting 'Disable'.

3\. Advanced Power Options

For desktop PCs, ensuring that power settings are optimized for performance can yield noticeable improvements.

To modify power settings: - Search for 'Power & sleep settings' in the Windows search bar. - Click on 'Additional power settings'. - Choose 'High performance'. If it’s not available, click on 'Create a power plan' and select 'High performance'.

4\. Enable Fast Startup

Fast Startup is a Windows feature that can help your PC boot up quicker. It's a hybrid of full shutdown and hibernation.

To enable Fast Startup: - Go to 'Control Panel', then 'Hardware and Sound', and finally 'Power Options'. - Click on 'Choose what the power buttons do'. - Click on 'Change settings that are currently unavailable'. - Under 'Shutdown settings', check the box for 'Turn on fast startup'. - Save changes.

5\. Consider Virtual Memory Optimization

Windows uses a paging file on your hard drive as virtual memory. Optimizing this can improve performance, especially on systems with limited RAM.

Steps to optimize virtual memory: - Open 'System Properties' as described earlier. - Under 'Performance', click 'Settings'. - Go to the 'Advanced' tab and click on 'Change' under 'Virtual memory'. - Uncheck 'Automatically manage paging file size for all drives'. - Select the drive where Windows is installed, usually C:, and choose 'Custom size'. - Set the initial size and maximum size to 1.5 times your installed RAM (for example, 6GB for a 4GB RAM system). - Click 'Set', then 'OK'.

Glary Utilities includes features like disk cleanup, registry repair, and memory optimization, which can help keep your system running smoothly without the need for manual intervention.

By implementing these advanced Windows optimization techniques, you can significantly enhance your system's performance, ensuring that it runs smoothly and efficiently for demanding tasks.
