Keeping your Windows PC running smoothly often comes down to smart cleanup and maintenance. One of the most effective ways to reclaim disk space and boost performance is by managing and deleting temporary files. Windows creates these files as you browse the internet, install programs, or use various applications. Over time, they can pile up and slow down your system. Thankfully, Windows offers built-in tools for cleaning them up, and third-party utilities like Glary Utilities make the process even easier and more thorough.

What Are Temporary Files and Why Should You Remove Them?

Temporary files are short-term files created to help your computer operate more efficiently. These might include leftover installer files, browser cache, and logs. While some of these files are deleted automatically, many linger and consume valuable disk space, potentially causing your system to run slower, produce errors, or reduce available storage.

Beginner: Using Built-In Windows Tools for Temporary File Cleanup

If you’re new to Windows maintenance, start with the utilities included in your operating system. Here’s how you can remove temporary files step by step:

Step 1: Open the Settings App Click the Start menu and select the gear icon to open Settings. Alternatively, press Windows + I on your keyboard.

Step 2: Navigate to System Storage In the Settings window, click on “System,” then select “Storage” from the sidebar.

Step 3: Access Temporary Files Under Storage, you’ll see a breakdown of how your space is being used. Click on “Temporary files.” Windows will scan your drive and show you a list of temporary files, including system-created files, downloads, and items in the recycle bin.

Step 4: Select Files to Remove Review the list and check the boxes next to the categories you want to clean. Be cautious with “Downloads” if you have important files in your Downloads folder.

Step 5: Remove Files After making your selections, click “Remove files” at the top. Windows will delete the files and free up space.

Practical Example: After a month of regular web browsing and Windows updates, many users find that up to several gigabytes of temporary files can be safely deleted using this method. It’s a straightforward way to prevent your storage from filling up with unnecessary data.
