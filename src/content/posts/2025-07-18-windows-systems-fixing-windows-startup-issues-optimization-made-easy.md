Windows startup issues can be frustrating, whether your PC boots slowly, displays error messages, or gets stuck on the loading screen. These problems can stem from corrupted files, unnecessary startup programs, or even malware. Fortunately, there are effective ways to diagnose and fix these startup issues, ensuring your Windows system boots up quickly and reliably. This article guides you through practical steps for cleaning up and repairing your Windows startup, tailored for both beginners and advanced users.

Why Do Startup Issues Happen on Windows?

Startup problems can arise from a variety of causes, such as:

\- Too many programs set to launch at startup - Corrupted or missing system files - Outdated or faulty drivers - Malware infections - Improper shutdowns or software crashes

A combination of cleanup and repair steps can help resolve these issues and optimize your PC’s boot time.

Step-by-Step Solutions for Beginners

1\. Disable Unnecessary Startup Programs

Unwanted programs launching at startup are a common culprit for slow boots.

How to do it manually: - Right-click the taskbar and select Task Manager. - Go to the Startup tab. - Review the list of programs. Right-click and choose “Disable” on any you don’t need at startup.

[Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) makes this even easier: - Open Glary Utilities and go to the “Startup Manager” tool. - Review the list of startup items. Uncheck or disable the ones you don’t recognize or don’t need.

2\. Run a Startup Repair

If Windows fails to start or shows errors: - Restart your PC and repeatedly press F8, F11, or Esc (depending on your model) to enter recovery. - Select “Troubleshoot” > “Advanced Options” > “Startup Repair.” - Follow instructions to automatically fix issues.

3\. Clean Up Junk Files

Junk and temporary files can slow down your system and affect startup. - Open Glary Utilities and use the “Disk Cleaner” feature. - Run a scan and remove unnecessary files safely.

4\. Scan for Malware

Malware can prevent normal startup. - Use Glary Utilities’ “Malware Remover” or your preferred antivirus to scan the system. - Quarantine or remove any threats found.

Advanced Solutions for Experienced Users

1\. Check Windows Event Viewer

For persistent or mysterious startup errors, the Event Viewer can help pinpoint causes. - Press Windows + R, type eventvwr, and press Enter. - Browse to Windows Logs > System. - Look for critical errors or warnings around the time of startup failures.

2\. Repair Corrupted System Files

Corrupted system files can block startup. Use System File Checker: - Open Command Prompt as Administrator. - Type sfc /scannow and press Enter. - Allow the scan to complete and follow any instructions.

[Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) also features a “Registry Repair” tool, which can fix registry errors tied to startup.

3\. Check for Driver Issues

Outdated or problematic drivers can cause startup hangs. - In Device Manager, look for devices with warning icons. - Update drivers manually or with Glary Utilities’ “Driver Manager” feature.

4\. Restore to a Previous State

If a recent update or installation caused the problem: - Go to Control Panel > Recovery > Open System Restore. - Pick a restore point from before the issue began and follow the prompts.
