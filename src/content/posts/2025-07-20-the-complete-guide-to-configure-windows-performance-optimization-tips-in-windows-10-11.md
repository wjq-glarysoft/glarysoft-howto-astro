Beginner Section: Easy Steps to Boost Performance

Is Your PC Slowing Down? Start with the Basics

1\. Disable Unnecessary Startup Programs

Many programs automatically add themselves to startup, slowing down boot time.

Steps: - Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Go to the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable programs you don’t need at startup.

2\. Remove Junk Files

Temporary files, caches, and leftover data can clog your hard drive.

Steps: - Open Settings > System > Storage. - Use Storage Sense to remove temporary files. - Or, use Glary Utilities’ Disk Cleaner for a deeper, automated clean.

3\. Keep Windows and Drivers Updated

Updates patch security holes and improve performance.

Steps: - Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update. - Click “Check for updates.” Glary Utilities can also help you check for outdated drivers and software, reducing manual effort.

4\. Adjust Visual Effects for Performance

Windows’ visual effects can look great but consume resources.

Steps: - Right-click This PC > Properties > Advanced system settings. - Under Performance, click Settings. - Select “Adjust for best performance” or manually uncheck effects you don’t need.

How Can You Further Improve Responsiveness?

1\. Defragment and Optimize Drives

Advanced Section: Fine-Tuning and Power Optimization

Ready for Advanced Performance Tweaks?

1\. Tweak Power Settings

Maximize performance by choosing the right power plan.

Steps: - Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Power Options. - Select “High performance” or “Ultimate performance” (on supported systems).

2\. Manage Services and Background Processes

Some Windows services run in the background and aren’t always necessary.

Steps: - Press Win + R, type services.msc, press Enter. - Research non-essential services before disabling. Only advanced users should change services!
