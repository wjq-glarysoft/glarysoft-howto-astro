Are you noticing your Windows 10 PC drains battery too quickly or doesn’t go to sleep as it should? The problem might be with how your power management tools are set up. Windows power management tools help control energy usage, prolong battery life, and keep your PC running smoothly. Over time, unnecessary settings, junk files, or misconfigurations can slow down your system or waste power. Here’s how beginners can clean, optimize, and troubleshoot Windows power management tools using simple system tools and applications like Glary Utilities.

Why Should You Clean Power Management Tools?

Power management settings are like the traffic lights of your computer’s hardware. If misconfigured, your PC may never rest, draining power and wearing out components. Cleaning and optimizing these tools ensures efficient energy use and a longer device lifespan.

1\. Review and Reset Power Plans

Windows comes with default power plans—Balanced, Power Saver, and High Performance. Sometimes, third-party apps or updates add unnecessary plans or alter existing ones.

To clean up: - Go to Settings > System > Power & sleep > Additional power settings. - Click on “Show additional plans” to see all available. - Remove or reset custom, unnecessary plans by selecting “Change plan settings” and then “Delete this plan.” - Restore defaults if unsure by choosing “Restore default settings for this plan.”

2\. Adjust Sleep and Display Timers

Unused or extreme timer settings can keep your PC awake or put it to sleep too often.

To adjust: - Go to Settings > System > Power & sleep. - Set a reasonable time for the screen to turn off (e.g., 5 minutes when plugged in, 2 minutes on battery). - Set sleep time for your comfort, usually 10-15 minutes on battery.

3\. Check for Unwanted Startup Programs

Startup programs can override sleep settings or keep your system unnecessarily busy.

To manage these: - Right-click the Taskbar and choose Task Manager. - Go to the Startup tab. - Disable programs you don’t need at startup.

4\. Clean Up Scheduled Tasks

Old or unnecessary scheduled tasks may prevent your PC from sleeping.

To clean: - Type “Task Scheduler” in the Start menu and open it. - Review Active Tasks. Look for tasks you don’t recognize or need. - Right-click and disable or delete as appropriate.

5\. Remove Unnecessary Device Wake Permissions

Some hardware (like network adapters or USB devices) can wake up your PC without reason.

To modify: - Open Device Manager (right-click Start > Device Manager). - Expand “Network adapters,” right-click your main Wi-Fi or Ethernet device, and select Properties. - Under the Power Management tab, uncheck “Allow this device to wake the computer” if not needed.

6\. Update Power Management Drivers

Outdated drivers can cause power issues.

To update: - In Device Manager, right-click devices like battery or network adapters. - Select “Update driver.”

8\. Scan and Fix System Errors

System file errors may disturb power tools.

To scan: - Open Command Prompt as administrator. - Type: sfc /scannow and press Enter.

9\. Optimize Battery Report and Usage

Understanding battery health helps you adjust power management settings.

To generate a report: - Open Command Prompt as administrator. - Type: powercfg /batteryreport and press Enter. - Navigate to the indicated path to view the generated HTML file.

Review the report for high-drain apps and adjust usage accordingly.
