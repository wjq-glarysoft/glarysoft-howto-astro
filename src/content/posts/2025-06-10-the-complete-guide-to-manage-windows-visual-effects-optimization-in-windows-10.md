Are you noticing slow performance on your Windows 10 PC, especially when opening windows, switching between apps, or running multiple programs? Visual effects, while making Windows look attractive, can sometimes impact system speed—especially on older or less powerful PCs. This guide walks beginner users through optimizing these effects for better speed and smoother operation, using easy-to-follow steps and practical examples.

What Are Windows Visual Effects and Why Do They Matter?

Windows 10 offers special visual effects like window animations, transparency, and shadow effects to make your desktop more attractive. These effects require additional computer resources, such as memory (RAM) and processing power (CPU). On newer or well-equipped PCs, this usually isn’t a problem, but if your system is lagging, adjusting these settings can deliver a noticeable boost in speed.

How Can You Access and Adjust Visual Effects?

1\. Open System Properties Right-click the Start button and select System. Click on Advanced system settings on the right. Under the Performance section, click on the Settings button.

2\. Adjust Visual Effects A new window called Performance Options will appear. You’ll see several choices: - Let Windows choose what’s best for my computer - Adjust for best appearance - Adjust for best performance - Custom

If you want the fastest response, select Adjust for best performance. This will turn off all visual effects. For a balance, choose Custom, and then tick only the effects you care about, such as “Show thumbnails instead of icons” (useful for quickly seeing image previews).

What Visual Effects Should Beginners Disable for Better Performance?

To start, consider turning off these common resource-heavy visual effects: - Animate windows when minimizing and maximizing - Animations in the taskbar - Fade or slide menus into view - Fade or slide ToolTips into view - Show shadows under windows Many users don’t notice these features once disabled but enjoy the improved speed.
