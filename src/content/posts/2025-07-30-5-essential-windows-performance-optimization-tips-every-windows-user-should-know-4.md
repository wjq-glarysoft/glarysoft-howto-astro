Is your Windows PC feeling sluggish or unresponsive? Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned user, optimizing your computer’s performance doesn’t have to be complicated. Why Does My Windows Computer Get Slow Over Time?

Over time, even the most powerful Windows PCs can become bogged down. Common culprits include too many startup programs, temporary files, outdated drivers, and unnecessary background processes. The good news is, with a little maintenance and the right tools, you can restore your PC’s performance quickly.

What Are the Most Impactful Startup Programs to Disable?

Many applications configure themselves to launch when Windows starts, which can significantly slow down boot times. Disabling unnecessary startup programs is one of the quickest ways to improve your PC’s responsiveness.

How to manage startup programs:

1\. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Click the Startup tab. 3. Review the list and right-click any program you don’t need at startup, then choose Disable.

For example, if you notice programs like Skype, OneDrive, or Spotify that you don’t use immediately, disable them for a faster boot.

How Do I Update Windows and Drivers for Better Stability?

Keeping Windows and your hardware drivers up to date ensures your system remains secure and performs optimally.

To update Windows:

1\. Press the Windows key, type Check for updates, and press Enter. 2. Click Check for updates and install any available updates.

To update drivers:

1\. Press the Windows key + X and select Device Manager. 2. Right-click any device (like Display adapters), choose Update driver, and follow the prompts.

Is It Safe to Uninstall Unused Programs?

Unused programs not only take up space but may also run background processes that slow down your PC.

How to uninstall safely:

1\. Open Settings (Windows key + I), go to Apps, then Apps & features. 2. Browse the list, select any app you don’t use, and click Uninstall.

How Can I Defragment My Hard Drive for Faster Access?

Fragmentation happens when files are split into pieces across your hard drive, making access slower. Defragmenting reorganizes data for speedier reads.

To defragment:

1\. Type Defragment and Optimize Drives in the Start menu and open the tool. 2. Select your main drive (usually C:) and click Optimize.

If you use a Solid State Drive (SSD), you don't need to defragment, but you can use the Optimize function to perform a TRIM, which helps maintain SSD speed.
