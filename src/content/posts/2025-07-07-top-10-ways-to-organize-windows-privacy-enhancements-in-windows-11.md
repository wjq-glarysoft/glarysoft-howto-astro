Windows 11 includes a broad range of privacy and security settings, but even experienced users commonly make mistakes that can leave personal data vulnerable. Organizing your privacy enhancements effectively can prevent data leaks, tracking, and other privacy concerns. This guide presents the top 10 ways to strengthen privacy in Windows 11, with practical advice, real-world examples, and clear sections for both beginners and advanced users.

1\. Understand and Review Privacy Settings

Beginner Tip: Start with the Windows Settings app. Press the Windows key + I, select Privacy & security, and explore each section. Common mistake: Skipping the review of default settings, which often enables more data collection than desired.

Advanced Tip: Dive into sub-menus like Diagnostics & feedback, Activity history, and App permissions. Manually disable anything you do not need.

Example: By default, sending diagnostic data to Microsoft is enabled. Reducing this to "Required diagnostic data" lessens the information sent from your PC.

2\. Limit App Permissions Smartly

Beginner Tip: Windows apps often request access to your location, camera, microphone, and contacts. Visit Privacy & security > App permissions and toggle off unnecessary permissions.

Common mistake: Granting blanket permissions during app installs. Always check what an app actually needs.

Advanced Tip: Use PowerShell to audit and restrict app permissions in bulk for enterprise environments.

Example: A weather app may need location, but a calculator app does not. Disable permissions accordingly.

3\. Organize Your Online Account Settings

Beginner Tip: Use a local account instead of a Microsoft account for sign-in if you’re concerned about cloud syncing and telemetry.

Common mistake: Linking every Windows feature to your Microsoft account by default, increasing data aggregation.

Advanced Tip: Use Group Policy Editor (gpedit.msc) or Registry Editor to further restrict syncing features and cloud activity logging.

4\. Disable Ad Tracking and Personalized Experiences

Beginner Tip: Go to Privacy & security > General and turn off "Let apps show me personalized ads" and related toggles.

Common mistake: Ignoring these toggles, which allows targeted apps and even Windows itself to collect more data for advertising purposes.

Advanced Tip: Block ad servers at the network level using the hosts file for even more privacy.

5\. Clean Up Activity History Regularly

Beginner Tip: Windows 11 tracks activity history to help you resume tasks. Visit Privacy & security > Activity history and clear this data periodically.

Common mistake: Letting months of activity history accumulate, creating a detailed profile of your habits.

Advanced Tip: Automate history clean-up with Task Scheduler or privacy tools.

6\. Use the Windows Security Dashboard

Beginner Tip: Open Windows Security via Start and explore the Privacy focus areas, such as App & browser control, and Device security.

Common mistake: Relying solely on default antivirus and not exploring further privacy protections like exploit protection and reputation-based protection.

Advanced Tip: Customize Exploit Protection settings and use Controlled Folder Access to protect sensitive files from ransomware.

7\. Harden Browser Privacy

Beginner Tip: Use private browsing modes and clear cookies and history after sessions.

Common mistake: Using Microsoft Edge or other browsers with default sync and telemetry turned on.
