Why Should You Clean and Optimize Your Disk?

Over time, your computer collects temporary files, leftover software fragments, and unnecessary data that can slow things down. Cleaning and optimizing your disk helps free up space, fixes minor errors, and improves your PC’s speed and stability.

What Are the Built-In Windows Tools for Disk Cleanup?

Windows includes basic tools to help keep your system tidy:

1\. Disk Cleanup Tool Open the Start menu and type “Disk Cleanup”. Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:), and the tool will scan for files you can safely remove. Check the boxes for things like “Temporary files” and “Recycle Bin”, then click “OK” to free up space.

2\. Storage Sense Go to Settings > System > Storage. Turn on Storage Sense to have Windows automatically delete temporary files and items in your recycle bin on a schedule.

What Are Some Common Mistakes Beginners Should Avoid?

\- Deleting system files you’re unsure about. Stick to the recommended settings in Glary Utilities and built-in Windows tools. - Skipping regular cleanups. When Should You Consider More Advanced Cleaning?

If your PC is still slow after regular cleanups, consider:
