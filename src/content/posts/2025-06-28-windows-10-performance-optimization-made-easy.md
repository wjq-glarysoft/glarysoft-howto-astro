Why Does Windows 10 Performance Slow Down?

Before diving into optimization, it’s important to understand the common causes of a sluggish system:

\- Unnecessary startup programs - Accumulated temporary files and system junk - Outdated or incompatible drivers - Background services hogging resources - Fragmented or cluttered hard drives - Malware or unnecessary browser extensions

Let’s tackle these issues with practical solutions.

For Beginners: Essential Optimization Steps

1\. Clean Up Your Startup Programs

Too many programs launching at startup can dramatically slow down boot times.

How to do it: - Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Click the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable non-essential programs (such as chat apps or third-party updaters).

Real-world example: Disabling cloud sync tools you rarely use can shave seconds off your boot time.

For Advanced Users: Deeper Optimization Techniques

1\. Manage Background Services

Some services aren’t essential and can be safely disabled.

How to do it: - Press Win + R, type services.msc, and press Enter. - Carefully review non-Microsoft services. - Right-click and set unnecessary services (like Fax or Remote Registry) to Manual.

Caution: Disabling critical services can destabilize Windows. Research services before making changes.

2\. Tweak Performance Options

Adjusting system visuals can free up resources.

\- Right-click “This PC,” select Properties, then Advanced system settings. - Under Performance, click Settings. - Select “Adjust for best performance” or customize which effects to keep.
