Understanding why system monitoring matters

Many Windows users notice their computer gradually slowing down or sometimes freezing without warning. This can be caused by unnecessary background processes, running out of disk space, or outdated drivers and software. Monitoring your Windows system regularly helps you spot these issues early, keeping your PC running smoothly and extending its lifespan. For beginners, knowing where to look and what to watch for is essential.

What tools does Windows offer for monitoring?

Windows comes with several built-in tools that help you monitor system performance:

Task Manager: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. Here, you can see which apps and processes use the most CPU, Memory, Disk, and Network. If your PC is slow, look for programs at the top of these lists and consider closing unnecessary ones.

Resource Monitor: From Task Manager, click on the "Performance" tab, then “Open Resource Monitor.” This tool offers a deeper look into how your system resources are being used, including disk and network activity.

Reliability Monitor: Search for “Reliability Monitor” in the Start menu. It displays a timeline of system events, crashes, and hardware changes, making it easy to spot patterns or repeated problems.

How can you act on what you find?

Suppose you notice your PC’s memory is almost always full. Open Task Manager and check the “Memory” tab. If you observe a browser with dozens of open tabs consuming most memory, close some tabs or restart the browser.

If your disk usage is high, especially at startup, it could be due to too many programs launching automatically. In Task Manager, go to the “Startup” tab and disable unnecessary apps from starting with Windows.

For repeated errors or crashes shown in Reliability Monitor, take note of the names or error codes. You can search online for solutions or check if the related software needs updating.
