Section 1: Getting Started with Windows Privacy (Beginner)

What are the Default Privacy Settings in Windows?

Windows 10 and 11 are designed to collect diagnostic and usage data by default. While some data collection improves the user experience, you may want to limit what is shared:

Step 1: Access Windows Privacy Settings 1. Click on the Start menu, then select Settings (the gear icon). 2. Choose Privacy & security from the left panel.

Step 2: Review Diagnostic Data Settings 1. Under Windows permissions, click on Diagnostics & feedback. 2. Set Diagnostic data to Required only, which sends minimal data to Microsoft.

Step 3: Manage Activity History 1. Click on Activity history. 2. Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” 3. Click “Clear” to erase previously stored activity data.

Step 4: Control App Permissions 1. Under App permissions, review sections like Location, Camera, Microphone, etc. 2. For each, toggle off access for apps you don’t trust or need.

Section 2: Intermediate Privacy Tweaks

How Do I Limit Cortana and Online Services?

Step 1: Disable Cortana 1. In Windows 10, right-click the taskbar, select “Cortana,” and choose “Hidden.” 2. In Windows 11, Cortana is not enabled by default but can be uninstalled through Settings > Apps.

Step 2: Minimize Online Speech Recognition 1. In Privacy & security, click on Speech. 2. Toggle off “Online speech recognition.”

Step 3: Limit Ad Tracking 1. In Privacy & security, click on General. 2. Toggle off “Let apps use advertising ID” and review other options to restrict personalized ads.

Section 3: Advanced Privacy Enhancements (Advanced Users)

How Do I Harden Privacy Using Group Policy or Registry?

For Windows Pro or Enterprise:

Step 1: Use Group Policy Editor 1. Press Windows + R, type gpedit.msc, and press Enter. 2. Navigate to Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > Windows Components > Data Collection and Preview Builds. 3. Double-click “Allow Telemetry” and set to “Disabled.”

For Home Edition:

Step 1: Edit the Registry 1. Press Windows + R, type regedit, and press Enter. 2. Navigate to HKEY\_LOCAL\_MACHINE\\SOFTWARE\\Policies\\Microsoft\\Windows\\DataCollection. 3. Create a new DWORD (32-bit) named AllowTelemetry and set value to 0.

What About Network Privacy?

Step 1: Set Network to Private 1. Right-click the network icon in the system tray and select Network & Internet settings. 2. Under Properties, set your connection as Private to limit device discovery.

Step 2: Use a Local Account 1. In Settings, go to Accounts > Your info. 2. Click “Sign in with a local account instead” to avoid syncing data to Microsoft.
