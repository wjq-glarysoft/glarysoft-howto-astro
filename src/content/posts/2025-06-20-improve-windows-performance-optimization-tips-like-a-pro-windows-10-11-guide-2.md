Beginner Tips: Quick Wins for a Faster Windows Experience

How Can I Reduce Startup Times Easily?

One of the fastest ways to improve performance is to limit which programs run at startup. Many applications sneak into your startup list, needlessly slowing boot times.

Step-by-step: 1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Click the Startup tab. 3. Review each item. Right-click and choose Disable for any non-essential program.

What’s the Easiest Way to Free Up Disk Space?

When your drive is nearly full, performance suffers. Regular cleanup removes junk files and frees up valuable space.

Manual steps: 1. Open Settings > System > Storage. 2. Click Temporary files. 3. Select files you don’t need (like Temporary files, Recycle Bin), then click Remove files.

How Do I Keep Windows Updated?

Microsoft regularly releases updates to improve performance and security. Out-of-date systems can lag or be vulnerable.

Step-by-step: 1. Open Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update. 2. Click Check for updates and install any pending updates.

Intermediate Tips: Next-Level Tweaks

How Can I Manage Background Apps?

Too many apps running in the background drain resources.

Step-by-step: 1. Go to Settings > Privacy > Background apps. 2. Turn off “Let apps run in the background” or disable individual apps you don’t need.

Alternatively, [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)’ “Process Manager” shows all active processes and resource usage, allowing you to quickly end unnecessary tasks or identify resource hogs.

What About Visual Effects—Do They Slow Down My PC?

Fancy animations and effects can look nice but tax older systems.

Step-by-step: 1. Right-click This PC and choose Properties. 2. Click Advanced system settings, then Settings under Performance. 3. Choose “Adjust for best performance” to disable all effects or selectively enable the ones you prefer.

Advanced Tips: For Power Users

How Can I Defragment My Drive or Optimize SSDs?

On traditional hard drives, fragmentation can slow access times. SSDs benefit from different optimization.

Step-by-step: 1. Open Start, type “Defragment and Optimize Drives,” and select it. 2. Choose your drive, click Optimize.
