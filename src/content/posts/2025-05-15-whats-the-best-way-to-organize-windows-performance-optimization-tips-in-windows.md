For Beginners: What Are the Basic Tips to Enhance Windows Performance?

1\. Keep Your System Updated Keeping Windows and all installed applications up to date ensures that you have the latest security patches and performance improvements. To do this, navigate to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update and click on 'Check for updates.'

2\. Manage Startup Programs Programs that start automatically when Windows boots can slow down your system. To manage these, press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager, then go to the 'Startup' tab. Here, you can disable programs that you don't need to start automatically.

3\. Use Disk Cleanup Windows includes a Disk Cleanup tool that helps to remove unnecessary files. Access it by typing 'Disk Cleanup' in the Start menu search box. This tool will help you reclaim storage space and improve performance by clearing temporary files and system caches.

4\. Defragment Your Hard Drive For traditional hard drives, defragmenting can optimize the storage space. Type 'Defragment and Optimize Drives' in the search box and follow the prompts to optimize your hard drive.

Intermediate Level: How Can Intermediate Users Further Optimize Windows Performance?

1\. Optimize Power Settings Adjust your power settings to balance performance and energy consumption. Go to Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Power Options, and select 'High performance' for better system speed.

2\. Disable Visual Effects Visual effects can consume resources unnecessarily. Type 'Performance Options' in the search box, select 'Adjust the appearance and performance of Windows,' and choose 'Adjust for best performance' or customize by disabling specific effects.

3\. Upgrade Your Hardware Adding more RAM or switching from a traditional hard drive to a Solid State Drive (SSD) can significantly boost system performance. This may require technical expertise, so consulting a professional might be recommended.
