Are you noticing your Windows PC running slower than usual? For many beginner Windows users, dealing with a sluggish computer can be frustrating. While Windows offers built-in tools for performance troubleshooting, common mistakes can lead to more problems or wasted time. This article guides you step by step through configuring Windows performance troubleshooting, highlights the most frequent mistakes beginners make, and shows you how to use practical solutions like Glary Utilities to make the process easier and more effective.

Why Is My Windows PC Slow?

Before jumping into troubleshooting, it’s helpful to understand what might be causing the slowdowns. Common causes include too many programs running at once, unnecessary startup items, low disk space, outdated drivers, and accumulated junk files. The good news is that most of these issues can be fixed with regular clean up and repair routines.

What Are the Most Common Troubleshooting Mistakes Beginners Make?

Mistake 1: Ignoring Regular Maintenance Many users expect their PCs to remain fast on their own. Skipping routine maintenance (like disk cleanup and defragmentation) leads to a gradual decrease in performance.

Mistake 2: Deleting System Files by Accident Beginners sometimes manually delete files they think are unneeded, which can cause problems. It’s safer to use reputable utilities that know what’s safe to remove.

Mistake 3: Disabling Essential Startup Programs Some advice suggests turning off all startup programs, but some are important for system health and security.

Mistake 4: Installing Too Many Optimization Tools Using multiple cleanup or repair tools at the same time can cause conflicts or redundant scans. Stick with one trusted tool.

Mistake 5: Ignoring Windows Updates Avoiding or delaying updates can keep your PC from getting important security and performance fixes.
