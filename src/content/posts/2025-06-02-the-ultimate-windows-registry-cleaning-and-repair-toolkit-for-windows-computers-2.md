When your Windows computer starts to slow down, freeze, or throw up mysterious errors, the culprit is often a messy, bloated, or corrupted Windows Registry. Why the Windows Registry Needs Maintenance

The Windows Registry is the central database for system settings, software configurations, and hardware information. Over time, installing and uninstalling programs, driver changes, and system updates can leave behind broken entries, obsolete data, and inconsistencies. These issues can cause slow startups, random error messages, and application crashes.
