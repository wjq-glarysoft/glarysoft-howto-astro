Windows 11 has brought numerous enhancements in terms of usability, design, and performance. However, with these advancements come newer challenges in maintaining privacy and ensuring security. For intermediate users looking to bolster their Windows 11 security and privacy measures, here's how you can effectively safeguard your system.

What Built-in Features Should You Leverage?

Windows 11 includes several built-in features designed to enhance your security and privacy right out of the box. Here's how to utilize them effectively:

1\. Windows Security: - Access Windows Security through Settings > Privacy & Security > Windows Security. - Ensure the Virus & threat protection is up-to-date. Run a quick or full scan regularly to catch any threats early. - Utilize the Account protection feature to secure sign-in options, including Windows Hello and PIN.

2\. Windows Firewall: - The Windows Firewall is crucial for blocking unauthorized access. Make sure it is enabled by navigating to Control Panel > System and Security > Windows Defender Firewall.

3\. BitLocker: - Protect your data from unauthorized access by encrypting your drives using BitLocker. To activate it, go to Control Panel > System and Security > BitLocker Drive Encryption, and follow the setup process.

How Can You Enhance Privacy Settings?

Beyond security, privacy is paramount. Windows 11 offers several privacy settings you can adjust to control data sharing:

1\. Manage App Permissions: - Visit Settings > Privacy & Security > App permissions. Review and control which apps have access to sensitive data like location, camera, and microphone.

2\. Diagnostic Data: - You can limit the amount and type of data sent to Microsoft by navigating to Settings > Privacy & Security > Diagnostics & feedback. Here, select the 'Required diagnostic data' option to minimize data sharing.

3\. Activity History: - To keep your activities private, turn off activity history storage by going to Settings > Privacy & Security > Activity history and ensure the option to store your activity history on this device is unchecked.

How Can You Implement Regular Security Practices?

Maintaining security and privacy on Windows 11 requires regular attention:

1\. Update System Regularly: - Always keep your system updated with the latest patches and security updates. Go to Settings > Windows Update and ensure automatic updates are enabled.

2\. Strong Passwords: - Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts. Incorporate a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols, and consider using a password manager for convenience.

3\. Enable Two-Factor Authentication: - Where possible, enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for all your accounts to add an extra layer of security beyond passwords.
