Why Does Disk Fragmentation Matter?

Fragmentation happens when files are divided into pieces and scattered across your drive. Over time, this makes file access slower, especially on traditional hard drives (HDDs). Regular defragmentation reorganizes data to improve performance. For SSDs, defragmentation isn’t needed, but optimization tools can still help with drive health.
