Understanding privacy settings in Windows 10 and Windows 11 can feel overwhelming, especially for beginners. However, taking control of your privacy is important to keep your data secure and to limit what information you share with Microsoft and other apps. This guide will walk you through the essential privacy enhancements, how to manage them, and introduce you to Glary Utilities for keeping your privacy settings in check.

Why Should You Care About Windows Privacy?

Windows collects information to personalize your experience and improve its services. However, not every user is comfortable sharing personal data or usage habits. By adjusting privacy settings, you decide what information stays private and what gets shared.

Where Can You Find Privacy Settings in Windows 10 & 11?

Both Windows 10 and 11 have a Privacy & Security section where you can manage most privacy-related settings.

1\. Click the Start menu and choose Settings (the gear icon). 2. In Windows 10, select "Privacy." In Windows 11, select "Privacy & security."

What Key Privacy Settings Should Beginners Adjust?

Location: If you don’t need apps to know your location, turn this off. - Go to Privacy Settings. - Click on "Location." - Toggle the "Location service" switch to off.

Camera & Microphone: Limit which apps can access your webcam and microphone. - In Privacy Settings, click "Camera" or "Microphone." - Disable access for apps you don’t trust or don’t use.

Activity History: Windows tracks your activities to help you pick up where you left off. You can limit this. - Go to "Activity history." - Uncheck "Store my activity history on this device" and "Send my activity history to Microsoft" if you prefer.

Diagnostics & Feedback: Control what information gets sent to Microsoft. - Under "Diagnostics & feedback," switch to "Required diagnostic data" instead of "Optional diagnostic data." - You can also clear diagnostic data here.

Advertising ID: Prevent Windows from creating a profile for personalized ads. - In the Privacy section, find "General." - Disable "Let apps use advertising ID to make ads more interesting to you."

How Can You Manage App Permissions Easily?

Windows allows you to control what each app can access (like contacts, photos, or messages).

\- In the Privacy settings, scroll down to "App permissions." - Review each category (Contacts, Calendar, Call history, etc.) and switch off access for apps that don’t need it.

How Do You Manage Privacy While Using Microsoft Edge?

If you use Microsoft Edge, you can adjust its privacy controls. - Open Edge, click the three dots in the top-right, and choose Settings. - Go to "Privacy, search, and services." - Set "Tracking prevention" to "Strict" for maximum privacy. - Clear browsing data regularly from the same menu.

How Do You Stay Safe When Downloading Apps?

Only download apps from the Microsoft Store or trusted sources. - Before installing a new program, read the privacy statement. - During installation, pay attention to permission requests.
