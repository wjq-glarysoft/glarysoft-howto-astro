As an advanced Windows user, you understand the significance of maintaining your system's performance. Troubleshooting Windows performance is an essential skill that can greatly enhance your productivity and system reliability. This article explores 15 proven methods to identify and rectify performance issues, providing you with practical, actionable advice.

Disk cleanup is often the first step in performance troubleshooting. Over time, your system accumulates unnecessary files that can slow performance. Startup programs can significantly impact boot times. Using Task Manager, navigate to the Startup tab to review and disable unnecessary programs. What Role Does System Resource Management Play?

Open Resource Monitor (accessible via Task Manager) to monitor CPU, memory, disk, and network usage. Identifying processes that consume excessive resources enables you to take appropriate action. Many services run in the background, often unnoticed. Use the Services app (services.msc) to manage these services.
