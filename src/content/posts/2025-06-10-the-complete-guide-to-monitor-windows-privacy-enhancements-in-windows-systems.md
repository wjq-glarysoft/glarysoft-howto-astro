Windows operating systems continue to evolve with new privacy enhancements, but effectively monitoring and controlling your privacy settings remains crucial. Whether you are a beginner exploring basic settings or an advanced user seeking granular control, understanding and keeping tabs on Windows privacy improvements can help you safeguard your personal data and maintain control over your system.

What Are Windows Privacy Enhancements?

Microsoft has introduced numerous privacy enhancements across recent Windows versions, such as Windows 10 and Windows 11. These enhancements include improved permission management, robust privacy dashboards, and more transparency on data collection. Key privacy features include granular app permissions, clear diagnostic data options, and streamlined privacy controls within system settings.

How Can You Access and Adjust Privacy Settings?

1\. Using Windows Settings

All users can access privacy settings directly through the Start Menu:

\- Open Settings - Go to Privacy & security

Here, you’ll find sections like Location, Camera, Microphone, Voice activation, and more. Review and toggle app permissions. For example, you can disable location access system-wide or just for specific apps.

2\. Reviewing Diagnostic Data

Windows collects diagnostic data for system improvement. You can choose between Basic (required) and Full (optional) data sharing:

\- Go to Settings - Select Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback - Choose your preferred data level

You can also view diagnostic data Windows collects by using the built-in Diagnostic Data Viewer app, which offers transparency on what’s sent to Microsoft.

3\. Managing Activity History

Windows can remember activity history to improve your experience across devices:

\- Go to Settings - Select Privacy & security > Activity history - Decide whether you want your activity history stored or sent to Microsoft
