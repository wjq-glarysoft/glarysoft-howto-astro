Windows 11 is designed to be sleek and efficient, but there are always ways to enhance its performance further. For beginners looking to make their system run smoother and faster without spending too much time, this guide will outline practical and time-saving tips for optimizing your Windows 11 PC.

Why Should You Optimize Windows 11?

Optimizing your PC ensures that it runs efficiently, reduces lag, and prolongs the lifespan of your hardware. By following simple tips, you can significantly improve your user experience without being a tech expert.

How to Manage Startup Programs?

One of the quickest ways to speed up your computer is to manage the programs that start up with Windows. Too many startup programs can slow down your boot time and consume resources unnecessarily.

1\. Right-click on the Start button and select 'Task Manager'. 2. Click on the 'Startup' tab. 3. Review the list of programs. Disable non-essential programs by selecting them and clicking 'Disable' at the bottom right.

Manage startup programs carefully. Essential programs like antivirus software should remain enabled, but apps like Spotify or Skype can be disabled.

How to Use Disk Cleanup?

Cleaning unnecessary files and freeing up disk space can enhance performance. Windows 11 includes a built-in Disk Cleanup tool that helps with this.

1\. Type 'Disk Cleanup' in the search bar and open it. 2. Select the drive you want to clean (usually the C: drive). 3. Click 'OK' and let Windows calculate the space you can free up. 4. Check the categories you want to delete, such as Temporary files and Recycle Bin contents. 5. Click 'OK' to delete the files.

How to Update Windows and Drivers?

Keeping your system up-to-date ensures you have the latest features and security improvements. Windows Update takes care of this for you:

1\. Open Settings by pressing Windows + I. 2. Go to 'Windows Update' and click 'Check for updates'. 3. Install any available updates.

How to Adjust Power Settings?

Power settings can affect your computer’s performance. For better performance, adjust these settings:

1\. Search for 'Power & sleep settings' in the search bar. 2. Click on 'Additional power settings'. 3. Choose 'High performance' from the list of plans.

This setting prioritizes performance over energy saving, making your PC faster at the cost of slightly increased power consumption.

How to Uninstall Unnecessary Programs?

Unnecessary programs can hog resources and slow down your PC. Regularly uninstalling these can help:

1\. Open Settings and go to 'Apps'. 2. Browse the list of installed programs. 3. Click on the program you want to remove and select 'Uninstall'.

Make sure to review programs you seldom use and remove anything that is not essential to your current needs.
