What is Disk Defragmentation?

For Beginners:

Disk defragmentation is essentially the process of reorganizing the data on your hard drive so that related pieces of files are stored closer together. This reduces the time it takes for your computer to read and write files, ultimately speeding up your system. Think of it like organizing a messy desk, where grouping similar items together helps you find what you need more quickly.

For Advanced Users:

From a technical standpoint, disk defragmentation decreases the number of fragmented files and condenses them into contiguous blocks. This reduces the head travel distance on the disk, minimizing the seek time and improving data throughput. It’s particularly beneficial for traditional spinning hard disk drives (HDDs), though less so for solid-state drives (SSDs).
