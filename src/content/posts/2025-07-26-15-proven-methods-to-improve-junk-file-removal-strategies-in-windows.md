Are you finding your Windows PC lagging, low on storage, or cluttered with useless files? Improving your junk file removal strategies is essential for maintaining system performance and freeing up valuable disk space. Here are 15 reliable methods, each with practical steps and examples, to help you efficiently clear junk files and keep your Windows computer running smoothly.

What Are Junk Files and Why Do They Matter?

Junk files include temporary files, log files, leftover data from uninstalled programs, cache, browser remnants, and obsolete system files. Removing them regularly prevents unnecessary storage consumption, reduces system errors, and can speed up your PC.

1\. Use Windows Disk Cleanup Tool

Step 1: Press the Windows key, type “Disk Cleanup,” and hit Enter. Step 2: Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:). Step 3: Check the file types you want to remove (Temporary files, Recycle Bin, Thumbnails, etc.). Step 4: Click “OK” and confirm deletion.

2\. Empty the Recycle Bin Regularly

Step 1: Right-click the Recycle Bin icon on your desktop. Step 2: Click “Empty Recycle Bin.” Step 3: Confirm the action.

3\. Delete Temporary Files Manually

Step 1: Press Windows key + R, type “%temp%”, and press Enter. Step 2: Select all files (Ctrl + A), right-click, and choose “Delete.” Step 3: Repeat for “C:\\Windows\\Temp” folder.

4\. Clean Up System Restore Points

Old restore points take up disk space. To remove them: Step 1: Open Disk Cleanup and select “Clean up system files.” Step 2: Go to the “More Options” tab. Step 3: Under System Restore and Shadow Copies, click “Clean up.”

5\. Clear Windows Update Cache

Step 1: Press Windows key + R, type “services.msc,” and press Enter. Step 2: Locate “Windows Update,” right-click, and choose “Stop.” Step 3: Go to “C:\\Windows\\SoftwareDistribution\\Download” and delete all files inside. Step 4: Restart the Windows Update service.

6\. Remove Browser Cache and Cookies

For Microsoft Edge: Step 1: Click the three-dot menu, select “Settings.” Step 2: Go to “Privacy, search, and services.” Step 3: Under “Clear browsing data,” click “Choose what to clear” and select cache, cookies, etc.

Repeat similar steps for Chrome and Firefox.

7\. Uninstall Unnecessary Programs

Step 1: Press Windows key + I to open Settings. Step 2: Go to “Apps” > “Installed apps.” Step 3: Review and uninstall programs you don’t need.

8\. Remove Leftovers from Uninstalled Software

Sometimes, uninstalled programs leave behind residual files. Step 1: After uninstallation, check “C:\\Program Files” and “C:\\Program Files (x86)” for leftover folders. Step 2: Manually delete unwanted folders.

10\. Clear Prefetch Files

Step 1: Press Windows key + R, type “prefetch,” and press Enter. Step 2: Select all files and delete them.

11\. Delete Old Downloaded Files

Step 1: Open the Downloads folder. Step 2: Sort files by date or type. Step 3: Delete anything you no longer need.

12\. Clean Up Startup Programs

Step 1: Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. Step 2: Go to the “Startup” tab. Step 3: Disable unnecessary startup items to prevent temporary files from unused apps.

13\. Remove Old User Profiles

Step 1: Press Windows key + R, type “sysdm.cpl,” and press Enter. Step 2: Go to the “Advanced” tab and click “Settings” under User Profiles. Step 3: Delete profiles not in use.

14\. Use Storage Sense for Automated Cleaning

Step 1: Go to Settings > System > Storage. Step 2: Turn on Storage Sense. Step 3: Configure it to delete temporary files and empty the Recycle Bin automatically.

15\. Regularly Check for Large, Obsolete Files

Step 1: Use File Explorer’s search with filters like “size:>100MB.” Step 2: Review and delete unnecessary large files.

How Often Should You Remove Junk Files?

For most users, a monthly cleanup is sufficient. However, heavy users or those with limited storage may benefit from weekly or even daily maintenance. Glary Utilities can schedule cleanups for convenience.

What Are the Risks of Deleting Junk Files?
