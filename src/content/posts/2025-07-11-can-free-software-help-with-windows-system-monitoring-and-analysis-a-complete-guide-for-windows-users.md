What is Windows System Monitoring and Why Does it Matter?

System monitoring means keeping an eye on your computer’s key components—such as CPU, memory, disk, and network usage. Analysis goes a step further, helping you figure out why your PC might be running slowly, overheating, or experiencing glitches. Monitoring lets you catch problems early, optimize performance, and prolong your hardware’s life.

For example, you might notice your system is slow every time you open multiple browser tabs. Monitoring can show if your memory or CPU is maxed out, suggesting it’s time to close unused programs or upgrade hardware in the future.
