Regularly optimizing and maintaining your Windows 10 or 11 system is the key to keeping your computer running smoothly, quickly, and securely. As a beginner, you might feel overwhelmed by complicated settings and technical jargon. This guide will walk you step-by-step through the most effective ways to monitor, optimize, and maintain your PC using simple built-in tools—and introduce Glary Utilities for even more convenience.

Why Should You Monitor and Maintain Your System?

Over time, your computer can slow down due to unnecessary files, background programs, and software issues. Regular maintenance helps: - Improve performance and speed - Prevent crashes and system errors - Extend the life of your computer

How Can You Check Your Computer’s Performance?

Checking your system’s current performance is the first step. Here’s how you can do it:

1\. Open Task Manager: - Right-click the taskbar and select Task Manager. - Click ‘More details’ if it opens in the compact view.

2\. Navigate to the Performance Tab: - Click the ‘Performance’ tab to see real-time graphs of your CPU, memory, disk, and network usage.

3\. Identify Resource Hogs: - Switch to the ‘Processes’ tab. - Click the ‘CPU’, ‘Memory’, or ‘Disk’ headers to sort the list and see which apps are using the most resources.

This helps you spot apps or processes that might be slowing your computer.

What Basic Maintenance Tasks Should You Do?

There are several simple tasks you can do regularly to keep your system optimized:

Run Disk Cleanup: - Type Disk Cleanup in the Start menu and open it. - Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:). - Check the types of files you want to delete (temporary files, Recycle Bin, etc.), then click ‘OK’.

Uninstall Unused Programs: - Go to Settings > Apps > Installed apps. - Click on programs you no longer use and select ‘Uninstall’.

Disable Startup Programs: - In Task Manager, click the ‘Startup’ tab. - Right-click on unnecessary programs and choose ‘Disable’ to stop them from slowing down your startup.

Update Windows and Drivers: - Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update. - Click ‘Check for updates’ and install any available updates. - For drivers, Windows Update often provides the latest versions, but you can also check the manufacturer’s website.
