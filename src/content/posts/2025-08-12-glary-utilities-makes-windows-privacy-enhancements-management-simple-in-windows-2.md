What Privacy Risks Should Windows Users Watch Out For?

Every time you use your PC, it records a wide range of data—recent documents, web browsing history, passwords, and even details about your hardware and software configuration. Telemetry and diagnostic data sent to Microsoft, third-party app permissions, and leftover files after uninstalling software can all affect your privacy. If not regularly managed, this information can be accessed by unauthorized users or applications, putting your privacy at risk.

How Can You Enhance Privacy Using Windows Built-In Tools?

1\. Adjust Windows Privacy Settings Go to Settings > Privacy & security. Review each section, especially “General,” “Speech,” “Activity history,” “Diagnostics & feedback,” and “App permissions.” Disable features you don’t use and limit data sharing to the bare minimum. For example, turn off “Send optional diagnostic data” and restrict which apps can access your camera, microphone, or contacts.

2\. Control Microsoft Account Syncing Under Settings > Accounts > Sync your settings, you can control what information is synchronized across devices. Turning off unnecessary sync options can help limit your data exposure.

3\. Clear Browsing and Activity History Most users know to clear browser histories, but don’t forget to sign out of your Microsoft account and clear your activity history at Settings > Privacy & security > Activity history. This removes traces of your searches and app usage.
