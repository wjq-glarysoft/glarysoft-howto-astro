Keeping your Windows system secure and your personal data private is increasingly important in today’s digital landscape. As an intermediate Windows user, you likely know the basics, but going beyond default settings can make a world of difference. Here are 15 actionable tips with real-world examples to help you enhance your system’s security and privacy.

Why Should You Regularly Update Windows?

Regular updates are your first line of defense. Microsoft frequently releases security patches for Windows. To ensure you’re protected against known vulnerabilities:

\- Go to Settings > Windows Update. - Click “Check for updates” and install all available updates. - Enable automatic updates for future protection.

How Do You Strengthen Your Password Practices?

A strong password is vital for all accounts, especially your Windows login.

\- Use a mix of upper/lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. - Avoid common passwords or using the same password across services. - Enable Windows Hello (face, fingerprint, or PIN) for an extra layer of security: Go to Settings > Accounts > Sign-in options.

What’s the Best Way to Enable Two-Factor Authentication?

Even with strong passwords, two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra barrier.

\- For Microsoft accounts, visit https://account.microsoft.com/security and turn on 2FA. - Use authenticator apps instead of SMS for better security.

How Can BitLocker Help Protect Your Data?

BitLocker encrypts your hard drive, making your data unreadable if your device is lost or stolen.

\- Search for “Manage BitLocker” in the Start menu. - Turn on BitLocker for your system and backup the recovery key in a safe place.

How Do You Tame Windows Telemetry and Data Collection?

Windows collects diagnostic data to improve user experience, but you can limit what is sent.

\- Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback. - Set “Send optional diagnostic data” to “Required only.” - Toggle off “Tailored experiences” and “Improve inking & typing.”

What Are the Best Practices for Firewall and Network Security?

Windows Defender Firewall helps block unauthorized connections.

\- Type “Windows Defender Firewall” in the Start menu. - Click “Turn Windows Defender Firewall on or off” and make sure it’s enabled for all networks. - Restrict public network sharing in Settings > Network & Internet > Wi-Fi > Manage known networks.

How Do You Safely Manage App Permissions?

Applications often request more access than necessary.

\- Go to Settings > Privacy & security > App permissions (such as Camera, Microphone, Location). - Review and toggle off permissions for apps that don’t need them.

What About User Account Control (UAC) Settings?

UAC prevents unauthorized changes to your system.

\- Type “UAC” in the Start menu. - Set the slider to “Always notify” for maximum security, or at least the recommended level.

How Should You Handle Suspicious Emails and Attachments?

Phishing is a common attack vector. Be cautious with all email attachments, even from known contacts.

\- If an email looks suspicious, verify the sender. - Do not click links or download attachments unless you’re certain they’re safe.

Why Is a Regular Backup Strategy Important?

Ransomware and other threats make backups essential.

\- Use Windows Backup (Settings > System > Storage > Advanced storage settings > Backup options) or another reliable backup solution. - Store backups offline or in the cloud, and verify them regularly.

How Can You Control Location Tracking?

Windows can track your device’s location, which may be unnecessary for many users.

\- Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Location. - Toggle off location services for your device or for individual apps.

What Steps Should You Take to Secure Your Web Browsing?

Browsers are frequent targets for attackers.

\- Use a privacy-focused browser like Mozilla Firefox or Microsoft Edge with tracking prevention enabled. - Regularly clear your browser history and cache—Glary Utilities’ “Tracks Eraser” can automate this task.

Why Should You Use Standard (Non-Admin) Accounts for Daily Tasks?

Using an admin account exposes your system to unnecessary risks.

\- Go to Settings > Accounts > Family & other users. - Create and use a standard account for daily activities, reserving admin access for system changes.

How Do You Monitor and Manage Connected Devices?

Unknown devices can introduce vulnerabilities.

\- In Settings > Devices, review paired Bluetooth and other devices. - Remove devices you no longer use or do not recognize.

What About Keeping Third-Party Apps Updated?

Outdated apps introduce security risks.

\- Use Glary Utilities’ “Software Update” tool to scan for outdated applications and update them with one click. - Alternatively, regularly check for updates within each app.
