What Is Disk Cleanup and Why Does It Matter?

Disk cleanup is the process of removing unneeded files from your computer—things like temporary files, cache, old updates, and items in your Recycle Bin. Over time, these accumulate and not only waste space but can also slow down your PC. Optimization goes a step further by making sure your data is stored efficiently so your computer can access information faster.

How Do You Use Windows’ Built-In Disk Cleanup?

Windows comes with a helpful tool called Disk Cleanup. Here’s how to use it:

1\. Click the Start button, type “Disk Cleanup,” and press Enter. 2. Choose the drive you want to clean, usually C:, and click OK. 3. Disk Cleanup will calculate how much space you can free up. It might take a minute. 4. Check the boxes for file types you want to delete. For beginners, it’s safe to select Temporary files, Recycle Bin, and Thumbnails. 5. Click OK, then confirm by clicking Delete Files.

Tip: Run Disk Cleanup every month to keep things tidy.

How Can You Optimize Your Hard Drive for Faster Performance?

Beyond deleting files, optimizing your drive makes your computer more efficient. For traditional hard drives, defragmenting rearranges files so Windows can access them faster.

To use Windows’ built-in tool:

1\. Click Start, type “Defragment and Optimize Drives,” and press Enter. 2. Select your hard drive and click Optimize. 3. Let Windows finish the process. For SSDs, Windows will only “trim” the drive, optimizing it safely.

How Do You Manage Programs That Slow Down Your Computer?

Many computers load unnecessary programs when they start up, which can slow everything down.

In Windows:

1\. Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. 2. Click the Startup tab. 3. Right-click programs you don’t need at startup and choose Disable.
