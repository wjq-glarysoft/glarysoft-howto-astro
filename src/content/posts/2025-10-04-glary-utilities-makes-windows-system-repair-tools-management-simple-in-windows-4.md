Why is managing system repair tools important?

Windows includes built-in utilities like Disk Cleanup, Defragment and Optimize Drives, and System File Checker (SFC). These tools can address specific performance or stability issues, but they lack integration and automation. Regular maintenance is necessary to prevent system slowdowns and potential crashes caused by registry errors, fragmented files, or startup program overloads. Managing these repairs through a central application helps maintain consistency, saves time, and reduces the chance of overlooking critical maintenance areas.
