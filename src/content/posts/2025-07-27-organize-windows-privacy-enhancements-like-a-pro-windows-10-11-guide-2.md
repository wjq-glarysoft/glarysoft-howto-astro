Every modern Windows PC holds a wealth of personal information, from browsing history and saved passwords to location data and app usage. For anyone concerned about digital privacy, Windows 10 and 11 offer a robust set of tools to help you regain control over your data. Whether you are a beginner or a seasoned user, learning to enhance your privacy in Windows is essential. Let’s walk through the most effective steps you can take right now.

Why Should You Care About Privacy Settings on Windows?

By default, Windows shares data to improve user experience and system updates. However, not all users are comfortable with this level of data sharing. Tightening your privacy settings can reduce targeted advertising, limit location tracking, and prevent unnecessary data collection. Plus, a more private system can help protect you against cyber threats and unauthorized access.

Which Privacy Settings Should You Start With?

1\. Control Diagnostic Data Collection

Microsoft collects diagnostic data to maintain and improve Windows. While some data is necessary, you can minimize what’s shared.

Step-by-step: - Go to Start > Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback. - Under Diagnostic data, select “Required diagnostic data” (Windows 10) or “Send required diagnostic data” (Windows 11). - Turn off “Tailored experiences” to stop Microsoft from using your data for personalized tips and ads.

2\. Manage Activity History

Windows can keep track of your app usage, websites, and files for “activity history.” While this can improve user experience, it also means storing more data.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Activity history. - Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” - Click “Clear” to erase already stored activity history.

3\. Disable Advertising ID and Personalized Ads

Windows assigns an advertising ID to each user, allowing apps to show personalized ads.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Privacy & security > General. - Turn off “Let apps use advertising ID to make ads more interesting to you.” - In your browser, consider enabling “Do Not Track” in privacy settings for an extra layer.

4\. Limit App Permissions

Apps often request access to location, camera, microphone, and more. You have control over what each app can access.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Privacy & security. - Under App permissions, review each category (Location, Camera, Microphone, etc.). - Decide which apps genuinely need access and toggle off permissions for the rest.

5\. Control Location Services

Windows location services can help with navigation and weather, but also track your movements.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Location. - Turn off “Location services” for the entire device or for specific apps. - Click “Clear” under Location history to remove stored data.

6\. Adjust Speech, Inking & Typing Data

Windows collects data to improve speech recognition and typing suggestions.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Speech. - Turn off online speech recognition. - For typing, scroll to “Inking & typing personalization” and turn off personalized suggestions.

7\. Review Sync Settings

If you use a Microsoft account, your settings and data may sync across devices.

Step-by-step: - Go to Settings > Accounts > Windows backup or Sync your settings. - Turn off “Sync settings” if you prefer to keep your data local.

What About Windows Updates and Privacy?

Regular Windows updates are crucial for security, but sometimes new features may introduce new data collection methods. Always review your privacy settings after major updates. Windows occasionally resets some privacy settings to their defaults, so it’s worth checking regularly.
