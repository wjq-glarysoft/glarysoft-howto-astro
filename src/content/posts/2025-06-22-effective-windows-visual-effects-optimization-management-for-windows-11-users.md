Are you noticing your Windows 11 computer running slower than you’d like? The visual effects in Windows 11, such as animations and transparency, add flair but can sometimes impact overall system performance, especially on older or less powerful PCs. Fortunately, with some simple adjustments, you can optimize these effects for a smoother experience without sacrificing essential functions.

What Are Windows Visual Effects and Why Do They Matter?

Visual effects include animations, shadows, transparency, and other graphical features that make Windows 11 look modern and appealing. While they make the system attractive, they can also use up system resources like CPU and RAM. This means your computer might slow down, especially if you have limited hardware.

Optimizing these effects is one of the easiest ways to improve performance, particularly for everyday tasks like web browsing, document editing, or even light gaming.

How Can You Adjust Visual Effects in Windows 11?

Windows 11 lets you manually configure which visual effects to keep and which to disable. Here’s how you can do it step by step:

1\. Open the Settings app by clicking the Start menu and selecting the gear icon or pressing Windows + I on your keyboard. 2. Go to System, then click on About. 3. In the Related links section, click Advanced system settings. This opens the System Properties window. 4. Under the Performance section, click Settings. 5. You will see the Performance Options window. Here, you have several choices: - Let Windows choose what’s best for my computer (Windows decides automatically) - Adjust for best appearance (turns on all visual effects) - Adjust for best performance (turns off most effects) - Custom (lets you pick and choose) 6. If you want the most performance, select Adjust for best performance. This disables most effects. 7. If you want a balance, select Custom, and then check only the effects you care about, such as “Show thumbnails instead of icons” or “Smooth edges of screen fonts.” 8. Click Apply, then OK to save your changes.

Which Visual Effects Should Beginners Consider Disabling?

If you want to keep things simple and fast, here are a few effects you might consider turning off:

\- Animate windows when minimizing and maximizing - Show shadows under windows - Show transparency in windows - Animations in the taskbar

By disabling these, you can speed up your computer without making it look too plain.
