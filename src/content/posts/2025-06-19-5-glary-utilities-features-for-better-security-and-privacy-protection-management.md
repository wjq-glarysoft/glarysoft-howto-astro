Windows users often focus on speed and usability, but security and privacy are just as critical in keeping your system safe from threats and unwanted exposure. Many users, even at an intermediate level, unknowingly make common mistakes—such as neglecting to delete sensitive browsing traces, forgetting about risky startup programs, or overlooking file deletion security. Glary Utilities is a powerful toolkit that helps you address these issues efficiently. Let’s look at five standout Glary Utilities features that can boost your privacy and security, practical advice on using them, and typical pitfalls to avoid.

Why Should You Care About Privacy and Security Mistakes?

Even if you use antivirus software, everyday habits can leave your system vulnerable. Examples include saving passwords in browsers, letting unused programs auto-start, or not properly erasing sensitive documents. Hackers, malware, or even curious family members can exploit these oversights. Glary Utilities provides easy-to-use tools to manage these risks without overwhelming you with jargon or complicated settings.

How Can You Use the ‘Tracks Eraser’ to Remove Sensitive Traces?

One of the biggest mistakes is assuming that deleting files or clearing browser history in the browser itself is enough. In reality, traces are left behind in multiple locations, including temporary files, cookies, and even auto-complete history.

[Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)’ Tracks Eraser goes beyond basic cleanup by targeting browser cache, history, cookies, and Windows system traces—all from a single dashboard.

Practical steps:

1\. Open Glary Utilities and select Tracks Eraser. 2. Check the boxes for browsers and applications you use (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Office). 3. Click ‘Erase Now’ to remove all selected traces securely.

Tip: Schedule this feature to run automatically after each session for ongoing privacy.

What Does the ‘File Shredder’ Do That the Recycle Bin Can’t?

Deleting files through the Recycle Bin or with Shift+Delete doesn’t actually erase the data. It remains recoverable with basic recovery tools, which is a common privacy risk. If you handle confidential documents, this oversight could leak sensitive information.

[Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)’ File Shredder permanently erases files by overwriting them, making recovery virtually impossible.

How to use:

1\. Launch Glary Utilities and open File Shredder. 2. Add files or folders you want to destroy. 3. Select the shredding method (multiple overwrite passes for higher security). 4. Click ‘Shred Now’ to securely erase the data.

Tip: Use File Shredder for tax documents or any file containing personal or work information you no longer need.

Are Unwanted Startup Programs Compromising Your Security?

Many users allow unnecessary or even suspicious programs to run at startup, which can slow down the PC and open backdoors for malware. Even legitimate software sometimes tries to auto-start, collecting data or consuming resources.

Glary Utilities’ Startup Manager helps you identify and disable programs that start with Windows.

How to use:

1\. Open Glary Utilities and go to Startup Manager. 2. Review each entry; look for unknown or rarely used programs. 3. Disable any that aren’t essential (you can always re-enable them later).

Tip: Remove anything you don’t recognize or use. If in doubt, research the process before disabling.

What Can the ‘Privacy Cleaner’ Do for Everyday Windows Use?

Temporary files, clipboard history, and system logs can reveal a lot about your habits. Forgetting to clear these leaves a footprint for others to follow and can even help malware to profile your activity.

Glary Utilities’ Privacy Cleaner targets these lesser-known data stores.

How to use:

1\. Launch [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) and select Privacy Cleaner. 2. Choose the areas you want to clean (temporary files, clipboard, log files, etc.). 3. Start the cleaning process and review the report for what’s been removed.

Tip: Use this regularly if you share your PC or work with sensitive data.

Why Should You Use ‘Browser Assistant’ to Manage Extensions and Settings?

Browser toolbars, add-ons, and extensions are a common route for spyware and trackers to access your data. Many users forget to review these, especially after installing free software.

Glary Utilities’ Browser Assistant shows all installed extensions and lets you disable or remove suspicious ones.

How to use:

1\. Open Glary Utilities and navigate to Browser Assistant. 2. Review extensions, toolbars, and plugins for each browser installed. 3. Remove anything you didn’t install intentionally or that looks suspicious.

Tip: Regularly check this section, especially after installing new programs or browsers.

Neglecting privacy and security is often a result of misunderstanding what data is left behind or overestimating built-in Windows protection.
