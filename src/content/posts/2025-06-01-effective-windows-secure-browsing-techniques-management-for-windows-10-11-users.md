How Can You Strengthen Browser Security on Windows?

Secure browsing is vital for preserving your privacy and protecting your data from cyber threats. While Windows 10 and 11 offer built-in security features, employing advanced techniques ensures your online activities remain private and your system safe.

To begin, always keep your browser updated. Modern browsers like Microsoft Edge, Chrome, and Firefox rapidly patch security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates to reduce manual effort. Additionally, disable or remove unused browser extensions. Many extensions request excessive permissions or become targets for malicious actors. Regularly review installed extensions and only keep those from trusted sources.

Consider running your browser in a dedicated user account. Creating a standard (non-administrator) Windows account specifically for browsing limits the damage malware can inflict if your browser is compromised. This is especially effective if you often visit unfamiliar sites or download files.

What Browser Settings Should You Configure?

Adjusting your browser’s privacy settings offers another layer of defense. For example, in Microsoft Edge:

1\. Go to Settings > Privacy, search, and services. 2. Set the tracking prevention level to “Strict.” 3. Block third-party cookies. 4. Enable “Send ‘Do Not Track’ requests.”

Chrome and Firefox offer similar options under their respective privacy and security menus. Activate features like “Enhanced Tracking Protection” (Firefox) or “Safe Browsing” (Chrome).

It’s also wise to disable autofill for passwords and sensitive information. Instead, use a trusted password manager. Windows 10 and 11 support Windows Hello and built-in credential management, but third-party password managers like Bitwarden or LastPass can enhance your security further by generating strong, unique passwords for each site.

How Can You Use Advanced Tools for Browsing Privacy?

For increased privacy, consider running your browsing sessions inside Windows Sandbox (available on Windows 10/11 Pro and Enterprise). Windows Sandbox provides a temporary, isolated environment specifically designed for testing applications or browsing risky websites. All changes made within the sandbox are discarded after closing it, ensuring no traces are left on your main system.

Additionally, using a reputable VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your internet traffic and makes it harder for ISPs or attackers to track your online activities. Ensure your VPN doesn’t log user data and is known for strong security practices.

Why Should You Harden Your DNS and Network Settings?

Malicious websites can exploit DNS vulnerabilities to redirect you to harmful pages. Switching to secure DNS providers such as Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) or Google (8.8.8.8) adds a layer of protection. Both Windows 10 and 11 allow encrypted DNS queries (DNS over HTTPS):

1\. Go to Settings > Network & Internet > Ethernet/Wi-Fi > Hardware properties. 2. Set “DNS server assignment” to “Manual.” 3. Enter a secure DNS provider and enable encrypted DNS.

This helps prevent eavesdropping and man-in-the-middle attacks.

What Real-World Practice Increases Your Security?

Develop security-minded habits. Never ignore browser security warnings about untrusted websites or certificates. When shopping or banking online, always check for HTTPS and the secure lock icon in the address bar. For even greater safety, use a separate browser profile or an entirely different browser for sensitive transactions.

Periodically review your browser’s saved passwords and clear them if no longer needed. Backup essential bookmarks and settings before performing major cleanups or upgrades to avoid data loss.

Summary

By utilizing advanced browser configurations, isolating browsing sessions, employing secure DNS, and integrating cleanup tools like Glary Utilities, you can achieve a robust privacy posture.
