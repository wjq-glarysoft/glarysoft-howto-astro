Do you notice your Windows 10 or 11 PC taking longer and longer to get started? A slow startup is a common frustration, but the good news is that you can make your computer boot faster with a few simple steps. This beginner-friendly guide explains how to optimize your Windows startup, making your system feel fresh and responsive again.

Why Does Windows Startup Slow Down?

Over time, new programs and background processes add themselves to your computer’s startup routine. Each one slightly delays the time it takes for Windows to become usable after you power on your PC. Unnecessary startup items, leftover files, outdated drivers, and overloaded services can all contribute to the slowdown.

Which Startup Programs Can I Disable?

Many programs set themselves to launch at startup, but not all are essential. Examples of programs you can usually disable include:

\- Music players (e.g., Spotify, iTunes) - Cloud sync apps you rarely use (e.g., Dropbox, Google Drive) - Printer management tools - Game launchers (e.g., Steam, Epic Games Launcher) - Chat clients (e.g., Skype, Discord)

You should never disable essential system programs or security software (like your antivirus). If you’re in doubt, it’s best to leave it enabled or research the program name online.

How to Manage Startup Programs in Windows 10 & 11

1\. Right-click on the taskbar and select Task Manager. 2. Go to the Startup tab. 3. Review the list of programs. Windows rates their impact as Low, Medium, or High. 4. To disable a program, right-click it and choose Disable. 5. Restart your computer and see if startup is faster.

For beginners, this is the safest and easiest way to manage startup items.

What Else Can You Do for Faster Startup?

1\. Clean Up System Junk: Unwanted files can slow Windows startup. Use the built-in Disk Cleanup tool or the “1-Click Maintenance” feature in [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) to clear junk files, temporary files, and other clutter.

2\. Check for Windows Updates: An outdated system can be sluggish. Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update and install any pending updates.

3\. Scan for Malware: Malware can bog down startup. Ensure your antivirus is up-to-date and run a full system scan. Glary Utilities also includes a basic malware scanner.

4\. Disable Unused Services: Advanced users can use the Services app (services.msc) to turn off services they don’t need. Beginners should approach this step with caution and research before disabling anything.

5\. Upgrade Your Hardware: If you’re still struggling with slow startup, consider upgrading to a solid-state drive (SSD). SSDs offer much faster boot times compared to traditional hard drives.

How Often Should I Optimize My Startup?

It’s a good habit to review your startup items every few months or after installing new software. Periodic cleanup with Glary Utilities or Windows’ built-in tools will keep your system running smoothly.
