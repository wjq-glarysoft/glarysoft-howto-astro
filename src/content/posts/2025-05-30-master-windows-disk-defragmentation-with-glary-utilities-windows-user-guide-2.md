Why Should You Defragment Your Disk?

Fragmented files make your hard drive work harder to find data, resulting in slower system performance, longer boot times, and sluggish file access. Regular defragmentation optimizes file placement, speeds up your computer, and can extend the life of traditional hard drives.

Note: If you’re using a solid-state drive (SSD), defragmentation is unnecessary and not recommended, as SSDs don’t suffer from fragmentation in the same way and defragmenting can reduce their lifespan. This guide is focused on traditional spinning hard drives (HDDs).

Common Questions About Disk Defragmentation

Q: How often should I defragment my hard drive? A: For regular users, scheduling defragmentation once a week is sufficient. Heavy users (video editors, gamers, etc.) may benefit from twice-weekly defragments.

Q: Can I use my computer while [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) is defragmenting? A: It’s best to avoid intensive tasks during defragmentation. Light tasks are generally fine, but performance may temporarily drop during the process.

Q: Will Glary Utilities defragment my SSD? A: No, Glary Utilities automatically detects SSDs and disables defragmentation to prevent unnecessary wear.
