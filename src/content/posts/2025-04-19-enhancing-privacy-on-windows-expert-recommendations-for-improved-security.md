In today's digital landscape, maintaining privacy on your Windows PC is more critical than ever. Windows, with its robust features, offers numerous options to safeguard your data and enhance security. This article will guide you through practical, actionable steps to bolster your privacy on Windows.

1\. Use a Local Account

One of the simplest ways to increase privacy is by using a local account instead of a Microsoft account. While a Microsoft account syncs your settings and files across devices, it also sends data to Microsoft's servers. To switch to a local account, go to Settings, select Accounts, then click on Your Info and choose Sign in with a local account instead.

2\. Modify Privacy Settings

Windows provides a range of privacy settings that you can adjust to limit data sharing. Navigate to Settings and click on Privacy. Here, you will find various options categorized under General, Location, Camera, Microphone, and more. For instance, you can turn off the advertising ID for apps, which can prevent personalized ads from being displayed based on your app usage.

3\. Control App Permissions

Similar to smartphones, Windows allows apps to access sensitive information such as your location, camera, and microphone. To manage these permissions, go to Settings, select Privacy, and review each category under App Permissions. For instance, you can disable location access for specific apps if you find it unnecessary for them to have that information.

4\. Use Windows Defender and Firewall

Windows Defender and the built-in firewall provide essential protection against malware and unauthorized access. Ensure Windows Defender is up-to-date and actively scanning your PC. Access Windows Security through Settings, click on Virus & threat protection, and check for updates. Also, verify that your firewall is enabled by visiting Network & Internet settings and selecting Firewall & network protection.

5\. Enable BitLocker Drive Encryption

To protect your data from unauthorized access, enable BitLocker on your Windows PC. BitLocker encrypts your drive, making it inaccessible without the correct authentication. To turn on BitLocker, search for BitLocker in the Start menu, select Manage BitLocker, and follow the instructions to encrypt your drive.

6\. Regularly Review Your Online Accounts

Regularly reviewing and managing your online accounts can prevent data breaches. Use a password manager to create and store strong, unique passwords for each account. Additionally, enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible to add an extra layer of security.

8\. Configure Windows Update Settings

Keeping your Windows operating system up-to-date is crucial for security. However, you may want to manage how updates are installed. Go to Settings, click on Update & Security, and then select Windows Update. Here, you can choose to Pause updates temporarily or set active hours to prevent updates from interrupting your work.

9\. Restrict Background Data Usage

Some apps continue to transmit data even when not in active use. To restrict background data usage, go to Settings, select Network & Internet, and click on Data usage. Here, you can set data limits and monitor the usage of different applications.
