Are you concerned about your privacy when using your Windows computer? You’re not alone! Windows offers many features and settings to help keep your data safe and secure, but knowing where to start can be overwhelming. This guide will walk you, as a beginner, through practical steps and easy-to-follow instructions to enhance your privacy and security on Windows.

What Are the Basics of Windows Privacy Settings?

Windows collects some information to help improve your experience, but you can control much of what is shared. Start by checking your privacy settings:

1\. Open the Start Menu and select Settings (the gear icon). 2. Click on Privacy & security (in Windows 11) or simply Privacy (in Windows 10). 3. Go through each section on the left sidebar such as General, Speech, Inking & typing personalization, Diagnostics & feedback, Activity history, and App permissions.

For example, under Diagnostics & feedback, switch from “Full” to “Required only” (Windows 10) or “Send optional diagnostic data” to “Don’t send” (Windows 11) to limit the amount of data sent to Microsoft.

How Can I Limit App Access to My Data?

Many apps ask for permission to access your location, camera, microphone, or files. You can manage these permissions easily:

1\. In Privacy & security, scroll to the App permissions section. 2. Click on categories like Location, Camera, or Microphone. 3. Toggle off access for apps you don’t want to allow or adjust individually.

For example, if you don’t use your computer for video calls, you can turn off access to the camera for all apps.

Should I Disable Location Tracking?

Location services can be useful, but also raise privacy concerns. To turn off location tracking:

1\. Go to Privacy & security > Location. 2. Click the toggle to turn Location services off for your device.

If you want some apps (like Maps) to use your location, leave it on and control app-specific permissions instead.

How Do I Manage Your Activity History?

Windows can track the apps and files you use to help you pick up where you left off. You may prefer to keep this private:

1\. Go to Privacy & security > Activity history. 2. Uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” 3. Click “Clear” to delete past activity history.

What About Microsoft Account Privacy?

If you use a Microsoft account to sign in, visit https://account.microsoft.com/privacy to review and adjust your privacy settings online. Here, you can clear browsing history, manage ad settings, and control how Microsoft uses your data.

How Do I Use a Local Account Instead of a Microsoft Account?

A local account keeps your information stored only on your device. To switch:

1\. Go to Settings > Accounts > Your info. 2. Select “Sign in with a local account instead” and follow the steps.

Should I Keep Windows and My Software Updated?

Absolutely! Updates often include important security patches. To check for updates:

1\. Go to Settings > Windows Update. 2. Click “Check for updates” and install any available.

How Can I Use Windows Defender for Extra Security?

Windows Defender is built into Windows and provides real-time protection. To make sure it’s active:

1\. Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Windows Security. 2. Open Windows Security and check Virus & threat protection. 3. Ensure it says “No action needed.”

If you see any alerts, follow the prompts to address them.

How Can I Secure My Lock Screen and Passwords?

Always set a strong password or PIN for your account:

1\. Go to Settings > Accounts > Sign-in options. 2. Choose a method such as Password, PIN, or Windows Hello. 3. Follow the steps to set up a secure sign-in.

Additionally, set your computer to lock automatically when idle by going to Settings > Personalization > Lock screen > Screen timeout settings.
