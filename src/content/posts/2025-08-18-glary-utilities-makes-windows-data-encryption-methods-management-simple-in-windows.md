What data encryption methods does Windows provide? Windows includes two main approaches to encryption. The first is BitLocker Drive Encryption, which secures entire drives with strong encryption tied to your system hardware. BitLocker is very effective for protecting full partitions, especially on laptops that might be lost or stolen. The second is the Encrypting File System (EFS), which allows you to encrypt individual files or folders directly from their properties menu. EFS is useful when you only want to secure specific sensitive documents rather than locking down an entire drive.

While these methods are powerful, they can feel overwhelming. Configuring BitLocker requires administrative privileges and understanding recovery keys. Managing EFS often leaves users confused about where their encryption certificates are stored and how to back them up. For many everyday Windows users, this complexity leads to encryption being ignored altogether.
