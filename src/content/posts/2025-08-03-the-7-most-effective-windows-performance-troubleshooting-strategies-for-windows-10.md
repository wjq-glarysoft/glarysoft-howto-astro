Windows 10 is a powerful operating system, but even the best PCs can slow down over time. Whether you notice sluggish startup times, laggy programs, or your system freezing, there are proven strategies to get your computer running smoothly again. This article outlines seven beginner-friendly Windows performance troubleshooting steps, focusing on simple Clean Up & Repair techniques. Let’s get started!

1\. Why Should You Restart Your Computer?

Many performance problems are temporary glitches that a quick restart can solve. Restarting shuts down all running programs and system processes, clearing your computer’s memory. If you haven’t restarted your PC in several days, try this first. It’s the fastest fix for common slowdowns.

3\. What Is Disk Fragmentation and How Can You Fix It?

Over time, files on your hard drive become fragmented, meaning parts of them are scattered in different locations. This makes loading files slower. Modern computers with SSDs don’t need defragmentation, but if you have a traditional hard drive, it’s important.

Windows has a built-in tool:

\- Search for “Defragment and Optimize Drives” from the Start menu. - Select your main drive (usually C:). - Click "Optimize" to start the process.

4\. How Can You Manage Startup Programs?

Many programs automatically launch when Windows starts, which can drastically slow down your boot time. Disabling unnecessary startup items is an effective way to speed things up.

Here’s how to do it:

\- Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Click on the "Startup" tab. - Review the list. Right-click programs you don’t need at startup, then choose "Disable."

You’ll notice a faster boot-up right away. Alternatively, Glary Utilities offers a Startup Manager under "Advanced Tools," which makes it simple to review and disable unnecessary apps with just a few clicks.

5\. Should You Check for Malware and Viruses?

Malware and viruses can hijack system resources, causing significant slowdowns. Running a scan with Windows Defender is a must:

\- Open Windows Security from the Start menu. - Choose "Virus & threat protection." - Click "Quick scan" or "Full scan."

Glary Utilities also provides a Malware Remover tool as part of its suite, offering an extra layer of protection to catch issues that might slip past basic scans.

6\. Why Is Keeping Windows and Drivers Updated Important?

Running outdated versions of Windows or hardware drivers can cause performance issues or security risks. To check for updates:

\- Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update. - Click "Check for updates."

For drivers, Windows usually updates them automatically, but for graphics and network drivers, check your manufacturer’s website or use Glary Utilities’ Driver Updater tool to make the process easier.

7\. How Do You Uninstall Unused Programs?

Programs you no longer use still take up space and may run background processes that slow down your computer. Here’s how to remove them:

\- Open Settings > Apps > Apps & features. - Browse the list and select programs you don’t use. - Click "Uninstall."
