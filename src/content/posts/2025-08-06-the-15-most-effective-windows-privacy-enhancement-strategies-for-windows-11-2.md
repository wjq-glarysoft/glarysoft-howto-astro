Privacy is a top concern for many Windows 11 users, especially as more personal data is collected and processed by both the operating system and third-party applications. Whether you’re a casual user or an IT professional, there are practical steps you can take to reclaim your privacy and take control over the information your device shares. Below, we outline 15 effective strategies for maximizing Windows 11 privacy, with clear advice for both beginners and advanced users.

Beginner Strategies: Easy Steps to Protect Your Privacy

1\. Adjust Privacy Settings in Windows 11 Head to Settings > Privacy & security. Here, you’ll find a comprehensive dashboard for managing permissions and data collection. Review each section (like Location, Camera, Microphone, and Account Info) and disable access for apps that don’t truly need it.

2\. Limit Diagnostic Data Collection Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Diagnostics & feedback. Switch diagnostic data from “Full” to “Required only.” This limits the amount of system data sent to Microsoft.

3\. Turn Off Activity History Navigate to Settings > Privacy & security > Activity history and uncheck “Store my activity history on this device.” This prevents Windows from keeping track of your actions across apps and services.

4\. Manage App Permissions Under Settings > Privacy & security > App permissions, review access for sensitive hardware like your webcam, microphone, and contacts. Disable permissions for apps that don’t require them.

5\. Control Location Services At Settings > Privacy & security > Location, you can disable location services entirely or only for specific apps. This keeps your device from sharing your physical location unnecessarily.

Intermediate Strategies: More In-Depth Privacy Measures

6\. Use a Local Account Instead of a Microsoft Account When setting up or managing your Windows PC, choose a local user account instead of signing in with a Microsoft account. This reduces the amount of personal data synced to the cloud. Switch by going to Settings > Accounts > Your info > Sign in with a local account instead.

7\. Disable Advertising ID Microsoft assigns a unique advertising ID to your profile. Opt out at Settings > Privacy & security > General by turning off “Let apps show me personalized ads by using my advertising ID.”

8\. Restrict Background App Activity Prevent apps from running in the background by navigating to Settings > Apps > Installed apps, then selecting each app and adjusting its background permissions.

9\. Limit Cortana and Online Speech Recognition Go to Settings > Privacy & security > Speech and turn off “Online speech recognition.” If you don’t use Cortana, disable it to prevent voice data from being collected.

Advanced Strategies: Deep Privacy Control for Power Users

11\. Harden Telemetry with Group Policy Editor If you have Windows 11 Pro or Enterprise, press Win+R, type gpedit.msc, and go to Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > Windows Components > Data Collection and Preview Builds. Set “Allow Telemetry” to “Disabled” for maximum data minimization.

12\. Use PowerShell to Remove Bloatware Open PowerShell as an administrator and use commands like Get-AppxPackage \*appname\* | Remove-AppxPackage to uninstall unwanted apps, reducing the footprint of software that may collect or transmit data.

13\. Configure Firewall and Network Protection Go to Windows Security > Firewall & network protection. Set your network profile to “Public” for stricter rules. Use advanced settings to create inbound or outbound rules blocking non-essential apps from accessing the internet.

14\. Encrypt Your Data with BitLocker Protect sensitive files with device encryption. Type “BitLocker” in the Start menu and follow the prompts to enable disk encryption, preventing unauthorized access if your device is lost or stolen.
