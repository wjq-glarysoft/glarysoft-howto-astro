Why Does Windows Slow Down?

There are several reasons your Windows computer might be running slowly. Some common causes include:

\- Unnecessary startup programs - Temporary files and system junk - Fragmented or full hard drives - Unused or outdated software - Malware or unwanted browser add-ons

Understanding these causes is the first step towards effective troubleshooting and repair.

What Are the Basic Steps for Improving Windows Performance?

1\. Restart Your Computer It might sound simple, but restarting your computer can resolve many performance issues by clearing temporary glitches and system memory.

2\. Check for Windows Updates Keeping your system up to date ensures it runs smoothly and securely. Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update and click “Check for updates.”

3\. Clean Up Disk Space A cluttered hard drive can slow down performance. Start with Windows’ built-in Disk Cleanup tool: - Press the Windows key, type “Disk Cleanup,” and select the tool. - Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:), and click OK. - Check the boxes for files you want to remove (like Temporary files, Recycle Bin), then click OK.

4\. Remove Unnecessary Startup Programs Too many programs launching at startup can slow your PC’s boot time. - Press Ctrl+Shift+Esc to open Task Manager. - Click on the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable programs you don’t need at startup.
