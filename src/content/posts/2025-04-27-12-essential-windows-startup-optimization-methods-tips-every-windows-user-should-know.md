Optimizing the startup process of your Windows computer can drastically improve performance and save valuable time. Here are 12 essential methods to enhance your Windows startup experience, specifically tailored for beginner users.

What is Windows Startup Optimization?

Windows startup optimization involves streamlining the processes and applications that load when your computer turns on. By reducing unnecessary programs and tasks, your system can start up faster and run more efficiently.

1\. Disable Unnecessary Startup Programs

One of the easiest ways to speed up startup is by disabling programs that automatically run when your PC starts.

\- Right-click on the taskbar and select 'Task Manager'. - Go to the 'Startup' tab. - Review the list and disable any program that you don't need to launch at startup by right-clicking and selecting 'Disable'.

Glary Utilities offers a user-friendly way to manage your startup items.

\- Download and install Glary Utilities. - Open the application and navigate to 'Startup Manager' under the 'Optimize & Improve' section. - Easily disable or delay startup programs with just a click.

3\. Turn off Fast Startup

While Fast Startup is designed to reduce boot time, it can sometimes cause issues.

\- Go to 'Control Panel' > 'Power Options'. - Click 'Choose what the power buttons do'. - Click 'Change settings that are currently unavailable'. - Uncheck 'Turn on fast startup (recommended)'.

4\. Update Windows Regularly

Keeping Windows updated ensures that you have the latest security patches and performance improvements.

\- Open 'Settings'. - Go to 'Update & Security' > 'Windows Update'. - Click 'Check for updates'.

5\. Disable Startup Services

Some services start with Windows and aren't necessary for day-to-day use.

\- Open 'System Configuration' by typing 'msconfig' in the search box. - Navigate to the 'Services' tab. - Check 'Hide all Microsoft services' and disable unnecessary ones.

6\. Clean Up Your Disk

A cluttered hard drive can slow down startup times.

\- Type 'Disk Cleanup' in the search box and open it. - Select the drive you want to clean. - Check the boxes for the types of files to delete and click 'OK'.

7\. Use a Solid State Drive (SSD)

Consider upgrading to an SSD, as they offer much faster boot times than traditional hard drives.

8\. Adjust Virtual Memory Settings

Incorrect virtual memory settings can affect startup speed.

\- Right-click 'This PC' and select 'Properties'. - Click 'Advanced system settings'. - Under 'Performance', click 'Settings'. - Navigate to the 'Advanced' tab and click 'Change' under Virtual Memory.

9\. Scan for Malware

Malware can significantly hinder your computer's performance.

\- Use Windows Defender or a third-party antivirus to scan your computer.

10\. Remove Unnecessary Hardware

Devices connected to your PC can slow down startup.

\- Disconnect any external drives, printers, or USB devices that aren’t needed.

11\. Optimize Boot Order in BIOS

Accessing BIOS settings can optimize which devices your PC checks first during startup.

\- Restart your computer and enter the BIOS (commonly by pressing F2, F10, or DEL during startup). - Set the primary boot device to your main hard drive.

Glary Utilities can perform a comprehensive check and tune-up.
