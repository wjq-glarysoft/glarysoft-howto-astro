Why Should You Regularly Restart Your Computer?

One of the simplest and most effective solutions to many Windows errors is restarting your computer. Many users make the mistake of leaving their computers running for days or putting them to sleep instead of performing a full restart. When you restart, Windows refreshes its system files, clears temporary glitches, and terminates lingering background processes that could be causing errors.

Practical example: If your programs are running slowly or you see error messages like “Not responding,” try a restart first before attempting more complex fixes.

How Can You Safely Clean Up Junk Files?

Over time, your computer accumulates unnecessary files—temporary files, browser cache, and leftover data from uninstalled programs. Many errors are caused by outdated Windows versions or device drivers. Beginners often overlook system updates, thinking they aren’t necessary. However, updates often include bug fixes and security patches that prevent or fix errors.

Why Should You Use System Restore Points?

Beginners often skip creating restore points, but they’re a powerful way to recover from errors caused by recent changes. System Restore allows you to roll back your computer to a previous state if something goes wrong after installing new software or drivers.

To create a restore point manually, search for “Create a restore point” in Windows, then click “Create.” Glary Utilities can also assist by reminding you to create restore points before making major changes.

Practical example: After a failed software installation, you can use System Restore to undo the changes and fix the error.
