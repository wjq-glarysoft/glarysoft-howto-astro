Is your Windows PC running slower than usual, freezing, or showing occasional errors? Even as a beginner, you can take practical steps to troubleshoot and manage your computer’s performance. Why Does Windows Slow Down Over Time?

Every computer becomes cluttered with unnecessary files, outdated programs, and system errors as time passes. Routine activities—like browsing the web, installing or uninstalling software, and daily use—can leave behind junk files, registry errors, and even minor software conflicts. When ignored, these issues lead to sluggish performance, slow startups, and unexpected errors.

How Can You Easily Identify Performance Issues?

For beginners, the first step is to recognize common signs of system trouble:

\- Slow startup or shutdown times - Programs taking longer to open or respond - Frequent error messages or system crashes - Low disk space warnings - Increased fan noise (indicating high CPU usage)

Start by checking the Task Manager (press Ctrl + Shift + Esc) to see if any programs are using a lot of memory or CPU. This overview helps identify resource hogs or unnecessary background processes.

What Are the Most Effective Clean Up Tasks You Can Perform?

1\. Disk Cleanup

Windows includes a built-in Disk Cleanup tool:

\- Type “Disk Cleanup” into the Start menu and open the app. - Select the drive you want to clean (usually C:). - Check boxes for Temporary Files, System Cache, and Recycle Bin. - Click OK to remove these files.

This frees up storage and can resolve basic sluggishness.

2\. Uninstall Unneeded Programs

Old software and unused apps take up space and can run background processes:

\- Open Settings > Apps > Installed apps (or Programs and Features in older versions). - Review the list and uninstall anything you don’t use.
