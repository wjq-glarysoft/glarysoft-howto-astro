Windows 11 brings a modern look and new features, but even this latest version can experience slowdowns and performance hiccups. For beginner Windows users, troubleshooting performance issues might seem daunting. However, some simple clean up and repair methods can significantly boost your PC’s speed and stability, especially if you avoid a few common mistakes. This article covers the most effective performance troubleshooting methods for Windows 11, with practical advice and real-world examples tailored for everyday users.

Why Does Windows 11 Slow Down?

Over time, Windows 11 can become sluggish due to a buildup of temporary files, leftover software traces, background programs, and even small errors in your system settings. Many users make mistakes in their clean-up efforts, such as deleting the wrong files or overlooking easier solutions. Let’s explore what works—and what to avoid.

What Are the Most Common Windows Performance Troubleshooting Mistakes?

1\. Deleting System Files Manually Some users try to free up space by deleting random files in the Windows folder or the Program Files directory. This can cause system instability or even prevent Windows from starting. Avoid deleting files unless you’re sure what they are.

2\. Installing Too Many “PC Booster” Apps There are many apps promising instant speed boosts, but many are ineffective or even harmful. Stick to trusted solutions with positive reputations.

3\. Ignoring Updates Not updating Windows or drivers can lead to performance issues and security risks.

4\. Forgetting to Remove Unneeded Startup Programs Many programs automatically start with Windows, slowing down boot time, but users often forget to manage these settings.

5\. Not Regularly Cleaning Temporary and Junk Files Temporary files, browser caches, and software leftovers can accumulate quickly, but many users neglect regular clean-ups.

2\. Run Windows Disk Cleanup While [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) is more comprehensive, Windows’ built-in Disk Cleanup is handy for a quick clean-up.

\- Type “Disk Cleanup” into the search box. - Select your system drive (usually C:) and click OK. - Check the types of files you want to remove, such as temporary files and recycle bin contents. - Click OK, then “Delete Files.”

3\. Uninstall Unused Programs Unused apps can take up space and slow your system.

\- Open Settings > Apps > Installed apps. - Scroll through the list and uninstall programs you no longer use.

4\. Limit Startup Programs Too many startup programs can slow down boot time.

\- Open Task Manager by pressing Ctrl + Shift + Esc. - Click the Startup tab. - Right-click and disable programs you don’t need to start automatically.

5\. Keep Windows and Drivers Updated Updates often contain performance improvements and bug fixes.

\- Go to Settings > Windows Update and check for updates regularly. - For drivers, visit your device manufacturer’s website or use trusted tools like Glary Utilities’ “Driver Manager” to help keep them up to date.
