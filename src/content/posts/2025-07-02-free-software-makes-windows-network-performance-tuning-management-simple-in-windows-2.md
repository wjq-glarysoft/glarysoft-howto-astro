Why Does Network Performance Matter?

Network performance affects everything you do online: streaming, gaming, video chatting, file downloads, and more. If your Windows PC isn’t optimized for network speed, you could be missing out on a smoother, faster experience.

What Are the Main Factors Slowing Down Your Network?

Several factors can cause slow network performance in Windows: - Outdated network drivers - Misconfigured network settings - Background programs using bandwidth - Accumulated system junk and cache - Inefficient TCP/IP parameters

Step-by-Step: Easy Ways to Optimize Windows Network Performance

Step 1: Clean Up System Junk and Network Cache

Over time, unnecessary files and cached information can slow down your browsing experience.

\- Download and install [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com) (free version). - Open the program and select “1-Click Maintenance.” - Make sure “Temporary Files” and “Tracks Eraser” are checked. - Click “Scan for Issues,” then “Repair Problems.” This will clear out old files, making your network connections more efficient.

Step 2: Disable Unused Startup Programs

Many programs start when your PC boots, some of which continue using your network in the background.

\- In Glary Utilities, go to the “Startup Manager” under the “Optimize & Improve” tab. - Review the list of startup items. Turn off anything you don’t need. Common culprits include auto-updaters and cloud sync services.

Step 3: Update Network Drivers

Outdated drivers can cause connectivity problems and slowdowns.

\- In Windows, type “Device Manager” in the Start menu and open it. - Expand “Network adapters.” - Right-click your network device and choose “Update driver.” Keeping your drivers up to date ensures maximum compatibility and speed.

Step 4: Optimize Network Settings with [Glary Utilities](https://www.glarysoft.com)

\- Open Glary Utilities, go to “Advanced Tools.” - Find and select “Internet Explorer Assistant” or “Network Assistant” (names may vary based on Glary version). - Use the recommended settings for optimal speed, or follow the guide within the tool to safely tweak your browser and network configurations.

Step 5: Monitor Real-time Network Usage

Sometimes, a single app can hog your entire bandwidth.

\- In Windows, press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. - Go to the “Performance” tab, then click “Wi-Fi” or “Ethernet.” - Note your current network speed. - Switch to the “Processes” tab and click on “Network” to sort by usage. - If you see a program using a lot of data that you don’t need, right-click and select “End Task.”

Step 6: Restart Your Router and PC

This simple step often resolves temporary network hiccups. Restarting clears out minor glitches and refreshes your connection.
