Why is Disk Cleanup Important?

Beginners: One of the simplest yet effective ways to boost your PC’s performance is by regularly performing disk cleanup. Unnecessary files accumulate over time and can slow down your system. Windows has a built-in Disk Cleanup tool that helps remove temporary files, system cache, and other clutter.

Steps for Disk Cleanup: 1. Type "Disk Cleanup" in the Windows search bar and select it. 2. Choose the drive you want to clean and press "OK". 3. Check the file types you wish to delete and click "OK".

Are You Neglecting Your Startup Programs?

Beginners: Many users don’t realize that too many startup programs can significantly slow down boot times. These are applications that launch automatically when your computer starts.

How to Manage Startup Programs: 1. Right-click the taskbar and select "Task Manager". 2. Go to the "Startup" tab. 3. Review the programs and disable any unnecessary ones by right-clicking and selecting "Disable".

Advanced Users: Using Glary Utilities, you can manage startup programs more effectively with its Startup Manager feature. It provides detailed information about each program's impact on your startup time.

Steps with Glary Utilities: 1. Open Glary Utilities and go to the "Advanced Tools" tab. 2. Click on "Startup Manager". 3. Review and disable or delay unnecessary programs.

Is Your System Update Schedule Optimal?

Beginners: Keeping Windows and all drivers updated is key to performance and security. Windows Update usually handles this, but it’s worth checking to ensure all updates are applied.

How to Check for Updates: 1. Go to "Settings". 2. Select "Update & Security". 3. Click "Check for updates" and allow Windows to install them.

Advanced Users: Beyond the standard updates, ensure your drivers are up to date. Glary Utilities includes a Driver Manager that can assist you in finding and updating outdated drivers efficiently.

Steps with Glary Utilities: 1. Go to the "Advanced Tools" tab. 2. Click on "Driver Manager". 3. Scan for outdated drivers and update them.

Are You Overlooking Regular System Maintenance?

Beginners: Routine maintenance tasks like defragmenting your hard drive (if you're using an HDD) and running antivirus scans can make a big difference.

How to Defragment: 1. Type "Defragment and Optimize Drives" in the search bar. 2. Select your hard drive and click "Optimize".
